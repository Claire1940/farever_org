## Choosing Your Path: Classes in Farever Online

Before setting foot on Tutorial Island, you must select a class that fits your preferred playstyle. The game currently offers four distinct archetypes, each with unique weapon skills and progression paths. While the initial choice defines your starting abilities, the gear you find—such as spellbooks for Mages or heavy blades for Warriors—will further specialize your role.

| Class | Primary Role | Key Weapons | Playstyle |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Warrior** | Tank / Melee DPS | Greatswords, Shields | High survivability and close-quarters combat. |
| **Rogue** | Burst DPS | Daggers, Bows | Fast movement, high critical damage, and stealth. |
| **Mage** | Ranged Magic | Staves, Grimoires | Area-of-effect (AoE) spells and crowd control. |
| **Priest** | Support / Healer | Maces, Wands | Essential for group play; focuses on healing and buffs. |

For those who enjoy tactical positioning and high damage from a distance, the Mage is a standout choice. Mages utilize floating Grimoires (spellbooks) that allow for a variety of elemental attacks. As you level up, you will unlock skill points to upgrade specific spells, such as "Scorch" or "Pyro Ball," significantly increasing your damage output and utility in combat.

## Mastering Movement: Gliding and Exploration

Exploration is a cornerstone of the experience. Unlike many traditional MMOs where movement is restricted to walking or riding mounts, this game introduces a verticality that rewards curious players. The most important tool in your arsenal is the **Glider**.

To activate the glider, simply press the **Spacebar** to jump and then press it again while in mid-air. This allows you to traverse large gaps and safely descend from high peaks. Many chests and secret items are hidden atop tall trees or cliff edges that are only accessible via clever gliding maneuvers.

### The Obelisk System

As you explore regions like Meridian or Prime Valley, you will encounter glowing purple beams of light. These are **Obelisks**. Interacting with an Obelisk serves several purposes:
1. **Map Reveal:** It clears the "fog of war" and reveals points of interest in the surrounding area.
2. **Fast Travel:** You can fly between discovered Obelisks to save time.
3. **Teleportation Point:** Pressing the **Z** key will instantly teleport you back to the last Obelisk you attuned to.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Be careful when attuning to a new Obelisk. The "Z" teleport always takes you to the *most recent* one you touched. If you have unfinished quests in a previous zone, you may have to walk back manually if you haven't unlocked the flight path.

## Combat Mechanics and Ability Progression

Combat in the game is more than just clicking on enemies. It requires active participation, including dodging and chaining abilities. Most enemies have "telegraphed" attacks—visual cues like red circles on the ground (AoE) or specific animations—that indicate a heavy hit is coming.

### Advanced Combat Tips
*   **Dodging:** Use the **Control** key to dash away from incoming attacks. This is vital when fighting world bosses like "Blob Dylan" or the "Beach Watcher."
*   **Target Locking:** Once locked onto a target, your abilities will track them more effectively.
*   **Ability Chaining:** Certain skills can be "chained" by clicking your basic attack in a specific rhythm, leading to powerful finishers.
*   **Shielding:** At level 5, many classes unlock a defensive skill (like the Mage's Shield, bound to **E**) that absorbs damage while allowing you to continue attacking.

| Mechanic | Input | Effect |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Basic Attack** | Left Click | Fast, low-damage strikes that build energy. |
| **Special Skill 1** | 1 | High-damage or utility skill (e.g., pulling enemies in). |
| **Ranged Skill** | T | Long-range projectile or stun ability. |
| **Blink/Dash** | Shift / Ctrl | Quick repositioning to avoid heavy hits. |
| **Health Potion** | X / V / B | Consumes food or potions to restore HP over time. |

## Questing and the Codex

The questing system is designed to guide you through the lore and mechanics of the world. Early quests, such as "Claw and Order," introduce you to the combat loop, while others focus on the **Codex**. The Codex is a bestiary that tracks the creatures you have defeated. Some NPCs will require you to defeat "Sparkling" variants of common mobs—like Sparkling Slimes or Bees—to progress. These variants are slightly tougher but offer better loot drops.

### Secret Orbs and Hidden Loot
There are **99 Secret Orbs** scattered across the starting island of Scover. Collecting these orbs often involves platforming challenges or hidden caves. They are a primary source of experience and achievements. Additionally, keep an eye out for **Treasure Chests**. Some chests are locked behind "Magic Orb" puzzles, where you must find three nearby orbs to unlock the loot inside.

## Entering the Lost City of Ma: Your First Dungeon

Dungeons offer the highest challenge and the best rewards. The **Lost City of Ma** is the first major instance you will encounter. It features a scaling difficulty system: Beginner, Hard, and Heroic. 

### Dungeon Strategy: The Lost City of Ma
1. **Clear the Foes:** You must eliminate a set number of "Special Foes" (usually 11) before the final boss chamber opens.
2. **Utilize the Environment:** Look for "Water Cannons" or "Launch Pads" to reach higher platforms.
3. **The Turtle Bus:** In the central water area, a giant turtle acts as a moving platform. Use it to reach secret orbs and shortcuts.
4. **The Nepil Boss Fight:** The final boss, the Nepil, uses a shield mechanic. When the shield is up, focus on high-damage "breaker" spells. Stay behind the boss to avoid his frontal cone attacks.

> **💡 Tip:** Don't rush to the boss. Exploring side paths in dungeons often yields "Secret Points" that grant bonus rewards and better gear at the end of the run.

## Gear, Inventory, and Crafting

As you progress, your inventory will fill with various materials like Spark Dust, Spark Samples, and Marigold Petals. These are used at crafting stations or with the Guild Merchant to upgrade your weapons. 

### Managing Your Equipment
*   **Armor Comparison:** The UI provides a side-by-side comparison of your current gear versus new drops. Always equip items with higher "Vitality" or "Magic Damage" stats.
*   **Backpacks:** Early in the game, you can obtain a "Pouch" that increases your inventory size. This is a priority purchase from the Guild Merchant.
*   **Runes:** Rare drops like "Rune: Frost Infusion" can be used to modify your existing skills, adding elemental effects like slow or freeze.

## FAQ

**Q: How do I track my quests in Farever Online?**

A: Currently, quests are tracked via a "Suggested Activities" list on the right side of the screen and purple beams in the world. You can also talk to the quest-giving NPC again to hear the objectives. A more robust quest log is expected in future updates.

**Q: What happens when I die?**

A: If you fall in combat, you will respawn at the nearest "Respawn Point" or Obelisk. You may lose some temporary buffs, but you can use a "Death Stone" to teleport exactly where you died to recover any dropped items or continue your progress.

**Q: Can I change my class later?**

A: While your starting class defines your initial stat growth, the game's flexible weapon system allows you to experiment with different playstyles by equipping different weapon types, such as switching from a Grimoire to a Staff as a Mage.

**Q: Where can I find the 99 Secret Orbs?**

A: They are scattered throughout Scover Island. Look for them in high places (trees, mountain peaks) or hidden inside caves. Some are only visible when you are gliding near specific landmarks.
