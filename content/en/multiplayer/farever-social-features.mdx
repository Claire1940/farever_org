## The Return of the Persistent Lobby

One of the most significant complaints in modern shooters has been the "disbanding lobby" system, where players are kicked back to a main menu after every match. This prevents friendships from forming and kills the natural rivalry that develops over several games. In 2026, the developers have addressed this by making persistent lobbies a core component of the experience.

When you finish a match, you aren't immediately separated. Instead, the post-game lobby allows for a "Rematch" vote or the ability to "Stay as a Party." This design philosophy emulates the classic LAN party feel, where you could play with the same group of people for hours.

| Feature | Description | Benefit |
|---|---|---|
| **Pre-Game Lobby** | View player profiles, ranks, and cosmetics before the match starts. | Build anticipation and identify potential threats or allies. |
| **Post-Game Continuity** | Lobbies do not automatically disband unless players manually exit. | Encourages the formation of new friendships and long-term rivalries. |
| **Party Up Prompt** | A one-button prompt to join the group you just played with. | Simplifies the process of finding consistent teammates without manual invites. |
| **Lobby Chat** | Global text and voice chat enabled during the loading and voting phases. | Allows for "trash talk" or strategy planning before the action begins. |

> **💡 Tip:** Use the "Stay as a Party" feature after a win to gain a small XP bonus for consecutive matches played with the same group.

## Balancing SBMM and Social Continuity

A major hurdle for modern social features is Skill-Based Matchmaking (SBMM). While SBMM is designed to keep matches fair, it often forces lobbies to disband so the algorithm can "re-evaluate" player skill. The 2026 approach utilizes **farever social features** to strike a balance.

Instead of strictly prioritizing skill parity above all else, the system looks for "social clusters." If a group of players has stayed together for three or more matches, the game will prioritize keeping that group together, even if the skill gap widens slightly. This acknowledges that the fun of playing with a consistent group often outweighs the need for a perfectly balanced "sweaty" match.

| Matchmaking Priority | Priority Level | Outcome |
|---|---|---|
| **Connection Quality** | **Critical** | Ensures low latency and prevents lag-related frustration. |
| **Social Continuity** | **High** | Keeps groups together across multiple matches whenever possible. |
| **Skill Parity** | **Medium** | Attempts to find fair opponents without breaking up established lobbies. |
| **Wait Time** | **Low** | Prefers a slightly longer wait for a better social match. |
