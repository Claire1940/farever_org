## Getting Started: Your First Solo Steps

The very first thing any player should do upon entering the world of FarEver is secure their transportation. Mobility is king in solo play, allowing you to kite enemies and traverse large maps quickly.

1.  **Claim Your Free Mount:** Open the shop menu (Escape) and look for the free starter bundle. This contains five essential items, including your first mount.
2.  **Equip the Mount:** Navigate to your collections (default key **Y**), select the mount tab at the bottom, and equip your new companion.
3.  **Optimize Hotkeys:** Use the options menu to map your mount/dismount button to a convenient mouse button. The transition between gliding and riding is seamless, which is vital for escaping tight spots.

## Maximizing Your Farever Solo Experience

To succeed as a **farever solo** player, you must understand that your power is split 50/50 between your chosen class and the weapons you wield. Unlike some MMOs where weapons are mere stat sticks, in FarEver, your weapon defines half of your active toolkit.

### Class and Weapon Synergy
Every class (currently Warrior, Rogue, Mage, and Archer, with Monk and Druid arriving later in 2026) has unique skills. However, equipping a weapon grants a secondary set of skills. For example, a two-handed sword might provide three active skills and one passive, while a fire scepter grants a suite of ranged magical attacks.

| Feature | Class Skills | Weapon Skills |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Source** | Character Leveling | Equipped Weapon Type |
| **Quantity** | Fixed per Class | Varies (up to 4 per weapon) |
| **Progression** | Skill Points (XP) | Skill Points (XP) |
| **Customization** | Rune Slots | Arsenal Slot Passive |

> **💡 Tip:** When you see a green blinking plus sign on your UI, it means you have skill points to spend. Prioritize skills that create synergies, such as those that deal extra damage to slowed or stunned enemies.

### The Arsenal System
The Arsenal slot is a game-changer for solo players. By placing a weapon in your Arsenal slot, you can benefit from one of its skills even while using your primary weapon. This is the best way to add a "heal-over-time" or a defensive shield to a class that normally lacks sustain, like the Rogue or Mage. To switch the specific skill being used from your Arsenal weapon, hold **Control and Left Click** on the item to select a different ability.

## Progression and Map Completion

Leveling up in FarEver isn't just about grinding mobs. The game rewards exploration and thoroughness. As a **farever solo** adventurer, you should focus on two main XP sources: Map Objectives and the Codex.

### Map Objectives
When you open your map, you will see various question marks and grayed-out areas. These represent specific objectives. Completing all objectives in a region provides a massive XP injection. This is often more efficient than mindless grinding.

### The Monster Codex
The Codex (default key **L**) tracks your kills. Reaching specific milestones (e.g., 4, 8, or 20 kills of a specific monster type) grants bonus XP.
*   **Mini-Bosses:** Some Codex entries only require killing a single mini-boss.
*   **Efficiency:** Before leaving a zone, check your Codex to see if you are only one or two kills away from a major XP milestone.

| Milestone | Reward Type | Solo Difficulty |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **4 Kills** | Small XP Boost | Easy |
| **8 Kills** | Medium XP Boost | Moderate |
| **20 Kills** | Large XP Boost | Time Consuming |

## Essential Crafting and Economy

Inventory management can be a nightmare for the **farever solo** player. You will constantly find "junk" gear and crafting materials. Knowing what to keep and what to recycle is the difference between being rich and being broke.

### The Spark Recycler
Never simply discard your old gear. Once you reach the second map, locate the "Spark Recycler" in the village hub. Right-clicking your unwanted gear here converts it into essential materials for crafting and upgrades. These materials are much lighter and more valuable than the gear itself.

### Bag Space and the Bank
Visit the Guild Merchant early and often. He sells "Small Pouches" that add six slots each to your inventory. While they cost gold, the investment pays for itself by allowing you to stay in the field longer. The Guild Merchant also provides access to your Bank, where you should store items like Training Manuals until you are ready to use them.

### Best Solo Profession: Alchemy
While there are six professions to choose from, Alchemy is the most vital for solo play in 2026.
*   **Healing Potions:** You can craft Minor Healing Potions using vials (bought from the Guild Merchant) and materials found in the world.
*   **Sustain:** Since you won't always have a healer with you, having a stack of 100-HP potions is mandatory for tackling dungeon bosses alone.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Crafting higher-tier items requires "Crafting Points." These are a limited resource that regenerates over time, so spend them wisely on items you truly need for survival.

## Exploration Tips for the Lone Traveler

The world of FarEver is vertical and often hides secrets underwater. As you explore, keep these mechanics in mind to avoid unnecessary deaths.

### Obelisks and Fast Travel
Obelisks are your lifelines. They serve as respawn points, map reveals, and fast travel hubs. When you interact with an Obelisk, you can set it as your teleportation point. You can also "fly" between discovered Obelisks. Note that this isn't an instant teleport; you travel as a ball of energy across the sky, which is automatic but takes a few moments.

### Underwater Survival
There is no swimming in FarEver; instead, you walk on the seabed.
*   **The Blue Bar:** This represents your oxygen. If it reaches zero, you begin losing health rapidly.
*   **Double Jumping:** You can jump significantly higher while underwater, allowing you to reach hidden chests or escape enemies.
*   **Brute Forcing:** If you have enough healing potions or a strong healing skill from your Arsenal slot, you can actually "brute force" your stay underwater by out-healing the drowning damage.

## FAQ

**Q: Can I really play Farever solo and beat all the bosses?**

A: Currently, most early-game bosses and dungeons are designed to be soloable on Normal difficulty. As you move into Hard and the upcoming Heroic modes, you will need to optimize your Arsenal slot and Alchemy potions to survive.

**Q: What is the best way to get gold as a solo player?**

A: Collect "Lost Packages" found in the world. Right-click them to turn them into "Completed Packages" and hand them over to the Guild Merchant for 100 gold each. This is much faster than selling individual loot.

**Q: Should I upgrade my starter weapons immediately?**

A: It is generally recommended to save your enhancement materials for higher-level gear. The bonuses on early-game weapons (like +1 Faith) are very minor and won't significantly impact your **farever solo** progression compared to simply leveling up your skills.

**Q: How do I get a pet to follow me?**

A: You can claim a free pet in the shop, but you can also catch others in the wild. You must buy a Net from the Guild Merchant and equip it in your active weapon slot. Note that using a Net makes you vulnerable as it replaces your weapon, so only do this when the area is clear of hostiles.
