## Choosing Your Role for Group Play

Success in Sagarta begins at the character creation screen. While Farever allows for solo play, the class system is clearly designed with group synergy in mind. Each class brings a unique set of skills that, when combined, allow a group to tackle higher-level areas much earlier than a solo player could.

| Class | Primary Role | Key Mechanic | Group Utility |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Warrior** | Tank / Melee DPS | Combo Points | High physical damage and threat management. |
| **Mage (Sunling)** | Ranged DPS | Spark Generation | Powerful AoE burns and stuns. |
| **Priest** | Healer / Support | Prayer Novas | Burst healing and group-wide buffs. |

The Warrior excels at consuming combo points to deal massive physical damage, while the Mage (often referred to as the Sunling) channels powerful beams that generate "Spark." This Spark resource is vital for activating advanced conduits and skills. For those engaging in **farever group content**, having at least one Priest is highly recommended to manage the "Prayer" system, which can turn the tide of a difficult boss fight with a well-timed Nova.

## Exploring the World of Sagarta

The world of Sagarta is divided into nine realms, many of which were sealed during the Cryis Age. As a new generation of Sunlings, players must map the ruins and restore the balance between the Nightlings and Sunlings. 

Exploration is heavily tied to the Obelisk system. These structures serve as both fast-travel points and respawn anchors. When moving through the world with a group, ensure that every member interacts with an Obelisk to set their teleportation point. This prevents the group from being separated after a wipe in high-risk zones like the Crimson Ram territories.

> **💡 Tip:** Use the "Z" key to access the teleportation menu once you have discovered multiple Obelisks. This allows your group to quickly regroup at the nearest landmark before a dungeon run.

## Early Game Boss Strategies

Before entering formal dungeons, players will encounter "Island Tips" and "Beach Watchers." these are open-world bosses that serve as gear checks. Fighting these as part of **farever group content** makes the encounters significantly smoother, as one player can draw aggro while others focus on mechanics.

| Boss Name | Location | Key Drops | Strategy |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Blob Dylan** | Island Tip | Level 3 Armor | Avoid the edge to prevent falling; ignore stuns as he is immune. |
| **Beast Watcher** | Southern Beach | Intellect Boots | Keep distance from his ranged swipes; block his heavy leap. |
| **Nepsilon** | Nepid's Hideout | Ghost Claws / Trident | Stay opposite his tail; use shields during the tidal phase. |

When facing Blob Dylan, the first major hurdle for many, it is important to note that he is immune to most stun effects. Groups should focus on rotating their defensive cooldowns. For example, a Mage can apply a "Scorch" burn while a Warrior uses "Withstand" to block incoming physical slams.

## Dungeon Walkthrough: Nepid's Hideout

Nepid's Hideout is the first true test of **farever group content**. Located near the Lambda NPC, this dungeon requires players to eliminate 11 "Special Foes" before the final boss, Nepsilon, will emerge. 

### Clearing Special Foes
The special foes inside the hideout are often protected by bubbles or shields. To break these, your group must coordinate their elemental attacks. If a Mage uses a Frost Infusion, it can slow the enemies, allowing the Warrior to land a full combo point finisher. 

### The Nepsilon Boss Fight
Nepsilon is a large aquatic demon that uses a variety of area-of-effect (AoE) attacks. His most dangerous move is the Tidal Wave, which can knock players off the platform. Since there is no "swim upward" mechanic in deep water, falling usually results in an instant death and a respawn at the dungeon entrance.

1.  **Phase 1:** Nepsilon will swipe with his tail. Position the group on the opposite side of the tail's current orientation.
2.  **Phase 2:** He will cast a bubble shield on himself. All players should focus their highest DPS skills here to break the shield before he completes his channel.
3.  **Phase 3:** Nepsilon enters a rage state. Use the "Nature's Infusion" rune on your Spark Beam to provide 25% healing to yourself while dealing damage.

## The Codex System and Group Progression

One of the most unique aspects of Farever is the Codex. Every enemy type, from the lowliest crab to the "Sparkling Slime," has a Codex entry. Completing these entries provides permanent stat boosts and gold rewards. 

In **farever group content**, Codex progress is shared. If one member of your party kills a rare "Sparkling Bee," the kill counts for everyone in the vicinity. This makes group farming the most viable way to reach the "Completionist" milestones. To complete a standard entry, you typically need to defeat eight of a specific monster type. Higher-tier entries may require 40 or more kills, which is significantly faster with a full party of four.

## Skill Customization and Runes

As you level up (the current level cap for the beta areas is level 10), you will unlock Runes. These are modifiers that change how your skills function. In a group setting, you should tailor your runes to fill gaps in the party's utility.

| Skill | Rune | Effect | Best Use Case |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Spark Beam** | Frost Infusion | Slows enemy by 30% | Kiting difficult melee bosses. |
| **Spark Beam** | Nature's Infusion | Heals player for 25% | Solo survivability or off-tanking. |
| **Scorch** | Pyro Ball | Adds AoE burn | Clearing large packs of crabs or slimes. |
| **Shield** | Tidal Power | 12s Heal over time | Maintaining health during long dungeon crawls. |

> **⚠️ Warning:** Be careful when using the Spark Beam in crowded areas. It auto-targets the next closest enemy upon the death of your current target, which can accidentally pull extra "mobs" and overwhelm your group.

## FAQ

**Q: How do I unlock the first dungeon in Farever?**

A: You must speak with the NPC Lambda after reaching level 5. He will task you with entering the Nepid's Hideout to defeat Nepsilon. Ensure you have cleared the "Beast Watcher" quest first to have adequate gear.

**Q: Does farever group content scale with the number of players?**

A: Yes, enemy health and some damage values scale based on the number of players in the immediate area. However, the mechanical complexity remains the same, making groups generally more effective.

**Q: Can I share loot with my group members?**

A: Currently, most gear drops are individual (personal loot). However, crafting components like "Sticky Fin" and "Linen Cloth" can be traded between players to help everyone complete their "Completed Package" quests.

**Q: What is the best way to farm XP in a group?**

A: The most efficient XP farm is completing Codex entries for "Sparkling" variants of monsters. These enemies provide significantly more experience and often drop rare runes and crafting components.
