## Getting Started in Saigerta

The world of Saigerta is an ancient land filled with ruins, dangerous fauna, and mysterious magical artifacts. Upon entering the game, players are introduced to the Pilgrims of Marillem, a faction dedicated to mapping the region and restoring its lost glory. The early game focuses on establishing your presence by interacting with Obelisks—massive stone pillars that serve as both checkpoints and fast-travel hubs.

One of the most distinctive features of the game is its dual-mode approach. While the online multiplayer is the primary draw, a robust offline mode exists for those who prefer a solo journey or wish to practice mechanics without server latency. However, the true depth of the game is found in its cooperative play, where different classes must synergize their abilities to overcome high-level dungeons and world bosses.

### Essential Exploration Tools

Before venturing into deep caves or high mountain peaks, players must master the traversal system. Saigerta is designed with verticality in mind, encouraging players to look up as often as they look forward.

| Tool | Function | Acquisition |
|---|---|---|
| **Glider** | Allows for slow descent and aerial traversal over gaps. | Obtained during the "Welcoming Treasure" quest. |
| **Grappling Hook** | Pulls the player toward specific anchor points or high ledges. | Found in the early exploration phase of Frankie Valley. |
| **Obelisks** | Acts as a respawn point and fast-travel node. | Must be manually activated in each new zone. |

> **💡 Tip:** Use the Grappling Hook to reach hidden chests located on top of ruined towers. These often contain rare crafting components like Spark Samples or Diaphanous Wings.

## Mastering the Priest Class

In **farever coop**, the Priest functions as much more than a simple "heal bot." The class is designed for self-sufficiency and active combat participation. By utilizing a mix of "Prayers" and "Totems," a skilled Priest can maintain party health while dealing significant magic damage.

### Class Mechanics and Skill Chains

The Priest’s combat loop revolves around building "Fervor" and triggering "Judgment" novas. The following table breaks down the primary skills available to the class in the early-to-mid game:

| Skill Name | Type | Effect |
|---|---|---|
| **Smite** | Combo Finisher | The final hit in a basic attack chain; can jump to nearby enemies at higher ranks. |
| **Radiance** | AoE Burst | Heals nearby allies while damaging enemies within the radius. |
| **Tidal Totem** | Support | Summons a totem that pulses healing every 2 seconds. |
| **Judgment** | Nova | Activates all "Ready" prayers, dealing massive magic damage based on Faith. |

**Skill Progression Tip:**
When upgrading your Priest, prioritize the **Water Bolt** and **Tidal Totem** synergy. Higher ranks of Water Bolt have a chance to increase the duration of active totems, allowing you to maintain multiple healing zones simultaneously during intense boss encounters.

## Action Combat: Blocking and Dodging

The combat in Farever is a significant departure from the tab-targeting systems found in classic MMOs. It requires active movement, reflex-based dodging, and strategic blocking. Every enemy, from the lowly Green Slime to the massive Nepsid Whale, has telegraphs that players must learn to read.

### The Defensive Meta

*   **Blocking:** Holding the block button reduces incoming physical damage by a percentage (usually 40% base). However, blocking costs Poise. If your Poise is depleted, you will be stunned.
*   **Dodging:** A quick dash that provides invulnerability frames. This is essential for avoiding "unblockable" red-telegraphed attacks.
*   **Well-Timed Bonus:** Successfully blocking right before an attack hits provides a "Well-Timed" bonus, which often reduces the Poise cost and may trigger specific gear passives.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Some enemies, particularly crabs and aquatic monsters, have hitboxes that extend vertically. Even if you are hovering above them with a glider, you may still take damage if you are within their "aggro cylinder."

## Dungeon Strategy: Nepsid’s Hideout

Dungeons are the heart of the **farever coop** experience. These instanced areas are designed for parties of up to four players and feature unique mechanics that cannot be found in the open world. One of the first major challenges is Nepsid’s Hideout, home to the boss Nepsilon.

### Nepsilon Boss Fight Guide

Nepsilon is a multi-phase encounter that tests the party's ability to manage positioning and environmental hazards.

1.  **Phase 1: The Shield:** Nepsilon will surround himself with a water shield. Players must deal consistent damage to break the shield while dodging his "Water Burst" attacks.
2.  **Phase 2: Beam Rotation:** Nepsilon fires rotating beams of water. The party must move in a circle to stay behind the beams. This is the ideal time for the Priest to drop a Tidal Totem in the center of the arena.
3.  **Phase 3: Totem Eruption:** As Nepsilon's health nears 30%, he becomes more aggressive. Use the Priest's ability to "erupt" totems to deal a final burst of damage to close out the fight.

## Gear and Progression

Progression in Farever is tied both to character level and equipment rank. Items like the **Ghost Clams** (a two-handed Priest weapon) can be leveled up to unlock new properties, such as increased critical strike chance or reduced skill cooldowns.

### Key Stats to Watch

| Stat | Impact |
|---|---|
| **Faith** | Increases magic damage and the potency of healing skills. |
| **Fervor** | Enhances damage, healing, and shielding while reducing incoming damage. |
| **Poise** | Determines how much damage you can block before being staggered. |
| **Magic Penetration** | Allows spells to bypass enemy elemental resistances. |

Players can further customize their builds through the "Arsenal" and "Talent" menus. While the 2026 release has streamlined these systems, the depth remains high, with professions like Alchemy and Cooking providing essential long-term buffs.

## FAQ

**Q: Can I play Farever coop with friends on different platforms?**

A: As of the 2026 launch, cross-platform play is supported between PC and major consoles, allowing you to join the same server shards regardless of your hardware.

**Q: Is there a penalty for dying in a dungeon?**

A: In **farever coop**, dying in a dungeon usually sends you back to the last activated Obelisk or checkpoint. You do not lose your equipped gear, but you may lose some progress within that specific instance if the entire party wipes.

**Q: How does the shard system work?**

A: When you enter a zone, the game automatically places you in a "shard" with a small group of other players. This prevents overcrowding while still allowing for spontaneous social interactions and group formation.

**Q: Do I need a subscription to play?**

A: No, Farever follows a "buy-to-play" model. Once you purchase the game, you have full access to the online and offline modes, though cosmetic items may be available through an in-game shop to support ongoing development.
