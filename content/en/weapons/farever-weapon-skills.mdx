## The Core Mechanics of Farever Combat

Combat in Farever is best described as a hybrid between a traditional MMO rotation and "Souls-like" action. Every enemy in the world has a physical hitbox; if an animation swings through the space your character occupies, you will take damage. Conversely, manual movement and well-timed dodges are your primary defensive tools.

The most significant departure from genre norms is the total absence of mana. Instead, your resources are managed through:
*   **Cooldowns:** Every active ability, including your dodge and block, operates on a timer.
*   **Spark Meter:** A resource specific to the Mage class that powers passive "conduits" for extra damage or utility.
*   **Block Gauge:** A stamina-like bar that depletes when you hold a block. Performing a "Perfect Block" results in a parry, which regenerates this gauge faster.

### Weapon Skills vs. Class Skills

Your action bar is divided into two distinct halves. On the left, you find your **farever weapon skills**, which change entirely based on the item currently equipped in your main hand. These include your basic auto-attack chain and two or three unique active abilities. On the right side of the bar are your Class Skills, which remain constant as you level up your character, regardless of your equipment.

| Skill Type | Source | Purpose |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Auto-Attack** | Equipped Weapon | Basic damage; ends in a powerful "Final Combo Attack." |
| **Weapon Skills** | Equipped Weapon | High-impact abilities (e.g., Flamethrower, Totems). |
| **Class Skills** | Character Level | Utility and core rotation (e.g., Blink, Spark Shield). |
| **Arsenal Skill** | Secondary Weapon | A single ability pulled from a different weapon type. |

## Understanding the Sparkmaster Passive

For Mages, the Sparkmaster mechanic is the bridge between your gear and your raw magical power. Using any weapon skill or completing a "Final Combo Attack" (the last hit of your auto-attack chain) consumes Spark.

> **💡 Expert Tip:** Always keep your Spark meter above the middle threshold. When you consume Spark while the gauge is more than half-full, you automatically trigger your "Conduits"—free extra spells like magic shards or power buffs.

By slotting different conduits into your Sparkmaster interface, you can customize your passive output. A popular setup in 2026 involves running one "Power" conduit for stacking magic damage and two "Shard" conduits for consistent projectile pressure.

## The Arsenal System: Building Your Loadout

Unlocked at Level 7, the Arsenal system is perhaps the most flexible part of **farever weapon skills** customization. It allows you to equip a secondary weapon and "borrow" one of its abilities to use alongside your primary weapon's kit.

For example, if you enjoy the long-range devastation of the Fire Staff but find yourself vulnerable when enemies get close, you can put a pair of Fist weapons in your Arsenal slot. By selecting the "Uppercut" skill from the Fists, you can instantly swap to a melee strike to peel for yourself without losing your Fire Staff's passive bonuses.

### Arsenal Synergies to Try
1.  **Staff + Totem:** Use a Fire Staff for damage while having a Water Totem from the Ghost Clams for passive healing.
2.  **Grimoire + Wave:** Add a large Area of Effect (AoE) knockback to your basic spellbook kit.
3.  **Fists + Fire Blast:** Combine high-mobility melee combat with instant-cast fire bursts for a "Battlemage" playstyle.

## Starter Weapon Breakdown

Upon reaching Level 5, players typically receive a blue mystery cache containing their first major weapon choice. Choosing the right one depends on your preferred engagement range and utility needs.

### Radiant Staff (Fire)
The Radiant Staff is the premier choice for players who want high-volume AoE damage. Its primary mechanic revolves around the **Flame Bomb** passive.

| Skill | Effect | Key Upgrade |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Blazing Beam** | A channeled flamethrower that deals damage over time. | Rank 3: Makes every tick a guaranteed Critical Strike. |
| **Pyrolasm** | A massive explosion that increases magic damage taken by enemies. | Rank 3: Crits have a 10% chance to reset the cooldown. |
| **Flame Bomb** | Passive: Stacks on enemies; at 10 stacks, it explodes. | Rank 1: Reduces required stacks from 15 to 10. |

The synergy here is incredible: because Blazing Beam can be upgraded to always crit, and crits apply Flame Bomb stacks, a single channel of the beam can trigger multiple explosions in seconds.

### Ghost Clams of the Low Tide (Water)
If you prefer a supportive role or high survivability, the Ghost Clams are unmatched. These "halos" allow you to summon totems that heal allies and slow enemies.

| Skill | Effect | Strategic Use |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Tidal Totem** | Places a totem that heals 4-5 HP every 2 seconds. | Stand inside the radius to out-sustain elite mobs. |
| **Riptide** | A frontal cone water burst dealing high magic damage. | Use after a totem "slow" to deal 25% bonus damage. |

## Mage Class Skills and Progression

While your **farever weapon skills** provide your primary damage, your class skills provide the utility required to survive the game's difficult dungeons. In 2026, the Mage level progression unlocks several "reset" mechanics that allow for massive burst windows.

### Essential Mage Abilities
*   **Spark Beam (Lvl 1):** Your primary Spark generator. You should weave this into every rotation when your meter drops below 50%.
*   **Spark Shield (Lvl 3):** Absorbs damage equal to 40% of your max health. Since it has a 30-second cooldown but a 60-second duration, you can "double-stack" the shield before entering a boss fight.
*   **Blink (Lvl 5):** A short-range teleport. Use this to bypass physical hitboxes and reposition during enemy wind-up animations.
*   **Chrono Reset (Lvl 20):** A powerful 3-minute cooldown that fully restores your Spark and resets all weapon skill timers.

### Rune Customization
Every class skill can be modified using Runes found as loot drops. For instance, the **Resurgence Rune** for Spark Shield restores Spark to the caster when the shield is destroyed. Always check your skill menu (K) to ensure your runes are actively selected, as they do not equip automatically upon consumption.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Runes are permanent additions to your spellbook, but you can only have one active at a time per skill. Choose the one that complements your current weapon's weaknesses.

## Advanced Rotation Strategies

To excel in Farever's endgame, you must master the "weaving" technique. This involves using an auto-attack chain until the Final Combo Attack, then immediately canceling the recovery animation with a weapon skill, followed by a Spark Beam to replenish the resource you just spent.

### Example Radiant Staff Rotation:
1.  **Pyrolasm:** Apply the 8% magic damage debuff to the target.
2.  **Blazing Beam:** Channel fully to stack Flame Bombs and slow the target.
3.  **Final Combo Auto:** Proc your conduits (Shards/Power) while the enemy is slowed.
4.  **Spark Beam:** Regenerate the Spark consumed by the previous steps.
5.  **Instant Pyrolasm:** If your beam crits reset the cooldown, fire it again immediately.

## FAQ

**Q: How do I unlock more farever weapon skills?**

A: Weapon skills are tied to the weapon itself. To unlock more powerful versions, you must level up the weapon by using it in combat. Each blue-tier weapon has three ranks for each of its skills that can be upgraded as the weapon reaches higher levels.

**Q: Can I use melee weapon skills as a Mage?**

A: Yes. Through the Arsenal system unlocked at Level 7, you can equip weapons like Fists or Claws in your secondary slot and use one of their physical skills. This is highly effective for "Battlemage" builds that want to mix melee and magic.

**Q: What is the best way to generate Spark?**

A: The most reliable way to generate Spark is using the **Spark Beam** class skill. Each tick of the beam regenerates 12 Spark. While Spark does regenerate passively out of combat, weaving Spark Beam into your active rotation is mandatory for sustained damage.

**Q: Do weapon skills cost mana?**

A: No, Farever does not use a mana system. All **farever weapon skills** and class skills are limited only by their specific cooldown timers and, for Mages, the availability of Spark to trigger passive conduits.
