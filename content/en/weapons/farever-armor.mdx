<Section icon={Swords} title="Core Attributes and Weapon Scaling">

Before diving into the complexities of **farever armor** penetration, you must understand the foundation of your damage: Attribute Scaling. Every weapon and skill in the game scales off a specific primary attribute, indicated by icons in the skill description. For instance, a fist icon represents Strength, while other icons may represent Faith or Intellect.

If you are playing a Priest but using a weapon like the Glorious Sword which scales off Strength, your damage will be significantly lower than if you used a Faith-scaling weapon. The calculation is typically a base value plus a percentage of your primary attribute.

| Attribute | Primary Scaling Type | Core Benefit | Secondary Benefit |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Strength** | Physical Melee | Increases raw physical power | Minor Critical Bonus boost |
| **Intellect** | Magical Spells | Increases spell potency | Minor Critical Bonus boost |
| **Faith** | Holy/Support | Increases healing and holy damage | Boosts core Priest abilities |
| **Dexterity** | Ranged/Agility | Increases attack speed/dodge | Higher base dodge chance |

<Callout type="info" title="Attribute Priority">
Always prioritize gear that provides the specific attribute your weapon and main skills scale from. A high-level weapon with the wrong scaling is often worse than a lower-level weapon that matches your primary stats.
</Callout>

Mastery is another layer of damage that acts as a straight multiplicative boost. For example, the Prayer of Virtue on a Priest can provide a 15% boost to all damage while a shield is active. These masteries are calculated after your base attribute scaling but before enemy armor reduction is applied.

<Section icon={Shield} title="Farever Armor and Penetration Mechanics">

The way **farever armor** functions is through a diminishing returns curve. As your armor rating increases, each additional point provides slightly less damage reduction. However, the curve is generous enough that reaching 2,000 armor can reduce incoming damage by approximately 46.6%.

To counter high enemy defense, you must utilize Armor Penetration (for physical) or Magic Penetration (for magical). The scaling for both is linear: 6 points of rating equals 1% penetration.

| Armor Rating | Damage Reduction % | Effective Damage Taken |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **500** | 17.9% | 82.1% |
| **1,000** | 30.4% | 69.6% |
| **1,500** | 39.6% | 60.4% |
| **2,000** | 46.6% | 53.4% |

<Callout type="warning" title="The Hard Cap">
It is widely believed that 600 points of Armor Penetration (100%) is the hard cap. Beyond this point, you completely ignore enemy armor. Testing against zero-defense dummies shows that armor pen does not provide "bonus" damage if the enemy has no armor.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="The Versatility of Fervor">

Fervor is often considered the "king of stats" in Farever because it is a multiplicative damage boost that also improves your survivability. Unlike Armor Penetration, which only increases damage against armored targets, Fervor increases your raw damage output, your healing potency, and your damage mitigation.

The scaling for Fervor is 15 points of rating for every 1% increase. While this is "slower" than Armor Penetration (which is 6 per 1%), its multi-purpose nature makes it incredibly valuable for almost every build.

<Section icon={Target} title="Critical Hits and Mastery Bonuses">

Critical hits in Farever can be somewhat misleading. When you see a "150% Critical Bonus," it actually means a 50% increase over a normal hit, not a 150% increase. Base critical chance starts low and is increased by 1% for every 12.5 points of Critical Rating.

Generally, Critical Chance is the lowest priority for damage dealers. This is because every 2% of Crit Chance only equates to a 1% average damage gain (assuming the standard 50% bonus). Unless you have specific gear like "Thorn Lace" or talents that trigger on crits, you are better off investing in Fervor or Armor Pen.

| Stat | Rating for 1% | Average DPS Gain per 100 Points |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Armor Pen** | 6.0 | ~2.7% (Against 1,500 Armor) |
| **Fervor** | 15.0 | ~6.6% (Flat Multiplier) |
| **Crit Chance** | 12.5 | ~4.0% (Assuming 50% Bonus) |

<Callout type="tip" title="Mastery Synergies">
Mastery stats (Physical or Magical) provide straight multiplicative boosts. Always check your active buffs; for example, some classes gain a 15-25% mastery boost through skill upgrades or specific rotations.
</Callout>

</Section>
