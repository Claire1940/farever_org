<Section icon={Shield} title="The Priest: Divine Versatility">

The Priest is arguably the most flexible entry in the **farever class tier list**. Starting with a scepter, this class excels at balancing offensive pressure with life-saving support. Their unique Rosary passive allows them to cycle through various prayers, which are then unleashed using the Judgment special ability for explosive results.

| Skill Name | Level | Effect | Utility |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Faithful Winds** | Early | Massive movement speed area buff | Positioning/Kiting |
| **Radiant Verdict** | Early | AoE damage with a long-duration stun | Crowd Control |
| **Fervor** | 10 | Party-wide damage and healing increase | Group Buff |
| **Crusader** | 15 | Self-buff for damage and CDR | Solo DPS |
| **Beacon of Hope** | 20 | Stores damage/healing to explode later | Burst Heal/Dmg |
| **Miracle** | 30 | 50% CDR to Judgment and free prayers | Endgame Power |

The Priest's talent trees (Tank, DPS, or Healer) allow them to fill any gap in a party composition. At level 30, the Miracle state makes the Priest a powerhouse, granting a free prayer every 15 seconds and halving the cooldown of their most impactful ability.

<Callout type="success" title="Why Play Priest?">
If you enjoy being the backbone of a team while still having the tools to solo difficult content, the Priest is the optimal choice for your first character.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Swords} title="The Warrior: Unstoppable Force">

The Warrior is the definitive frontline specialist. Equipped with a sword and shield, they are designed to absorb punishment and control the battlefield through knockbacks and stuns. Their Rage mechanic rewards aggressive play, as every strike builds the resource needed for their devastating Raging Smash.

| Ability | Type | Key Benefit |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Cleave** | AoE | Deals damage and provides knockback |
| **Shield Bash** | CC | Reliable single-target stun |
| **Ignore Pain** | Defensive | **40% damage reduction** (Strongest in game) |
| **Battle Shout** | Buff | Increases party critical hit chance by 20% |
| **Berserk** | Steroid | Faster rage generation and higher damage |
| **Burst of Anger** | Utility | Removes cooldown from next 3 weapon skills |

The Warrior's talent trees focus on Critical Focus, Heavy Bleed, or pure Tanking. The ability to use Ignore Pain makes them nearly immortal in high-pressure situations, securing their spot at the top of the **farever class tier list** for 2026.

<Callout type="warning" title="Rage Management">
New Warriors often struggle with over-capping Rage. Ensure you are using Raging Smash frequently to maximize your AoE damage and keep enemies staggered.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="The Mage: Master of Sparks">

The Mage offers a complex but rewarding ranged playstyle. Utilizing the Spark Master passive, Mages can customize their spell effects by swapping spark slots. This allows for a high degree of adaptability, whether you need more crowd control or raw elemental damage.

| Key Skill | Description | Strategic Use |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Pyroball** | Heavy damage and burning debuff | Initial burst |
| **Fire Blast** | Instant damage and stun on burning targets | Interrupting spells |
| **Blink** | Directional teleport | Escaping boss mechanics |
| **Mystic Empowerment** | Party CDR buff | Maximizing team DPS |
| **Static Nova** | AoE damage and immobilization | Controlling adds |
| **Chrono Reset** | Regenerates sparks and abilities | Sustained combat |

At level 30, the Mage unlocks Overload, which generates random sparks every second. This leads to a chaotic but extremely high-damage window where the Mage can dominate the battlefield. Their talent trees include Spark Focus, Magic Blaster, and a surprising Healing tree, making them more than just simple glass cannons.

<Callout type="info" title="Spark Customization">
Always check your Spark Master setup before entering a dungeon. Swapping sparks to match enemy weaknesses is the hallmark of a professional Mage player.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Target} title="The Rogue: Shadow and Steel">

The Rogue is the premier choice for players who value mobility and precision. Starting with daggers, the Rogue utilizes a Combo Point system to fuel their Finisher ability. Their kit is loaded with teleports and utility skills that allow them to bypass enemy defenses and strike at vulnerable targets.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Generate Combo Points">
    Use basic attack finishers or abilities like Tempest Knives to build your resource bar.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Apply Death Mark">
    At level 10, use this cooldown to increase all damage dealt to the target by 30%.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Unleash the Finisher">
    Consume all points for massive damage. During the level 20 power spike, Finishers have no cooldown for 10 seconds.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Utility Coverage">
    Deploy Smoke Bomb to help your party dodge incoming attacks while repositioning with Shadow Step.
  </Step>
</Steps>

The Rogue's talent trees offer paths for Poison, Cooldown Reduction, or Utility. While they require more finesse than the Warrior, their ability to summon shadows at level 30 (Darkness Shadows) provides a significant boost to their sustained damage output.

<Callout type="tip" title="Backstab Bonus">
Skills like Shadow Jump automatically put you behind the enemy. Always prioritize attacking from the rear to maximize your natural Rogue advantages.
</Callout>

</Section>
