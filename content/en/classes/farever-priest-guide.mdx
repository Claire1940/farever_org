## Core Attributes and Stat Priority

The Priest relies heavily on the Intellect stat to boost both healing output and magical damage. Unlike the Mage, who focuses purely on destruction, the Priest must balance mana regeneration with raw power. In the current 2026 meta, prioritizing Intellect allows you to utilize higher-tier gear and unique artifacts that unlock at specific level milestones.

| Stat | Priority | Benefit for Priests |
|---|---|---|
| **Intellect** | Primary | Increases healing potency and spell damage. |
| **Spirit** | Secondary | Enhances mana regeneration and magic resistance. |
| **Stamina** | Tertiary | Boosts total health to survive dungeon boss "one-shots." |
| **Haste** | Optional | Reduces the cast time of long-duration heals. |

> **Warning:** Avoid over-investing in Strength or Agility. While Priests can equip some melee weapons, their primary scaling comes from magical attributes found on staves and wands.

## Choosing Your Crafting Job

In Farever, your "Job" defines your utility outside of combat. For a Priest, two main paths stand out: the Enchanter and the Alchemist. According to recent 2026 gameplay data, the Enchanter job is particularly potent for Priests because it allows for the creation of gear that specifically boosts Intellect.

To craft blue-tier armor or belts, you will need to visit a crafting station. For example, a common Priest recipe involves:
*   **Black Page:** A rare drop or vendor item used for magical inscriptions.
*   **Purified Bright Powder:** Essential for infusing gear with light energy.
*   **Copper Ore:** Standard base material for structural integrity.

If you choose the Enchanter path, you can trade your services with a Blacksmith. While the Blacksmith focuses on bending metal, the Enchanter focuses on the "power of plants" and magical extraction. This synergy is vital for clearing the recommended level 10 dungeons where gear requirements become significantly steeper.

## Leveling and Dungeon Strategy

Leveling from 1 to 10 is relatively straightforward, but the difficulty spikes once you reach the first major dungeon hubs. Most players find that soloing these areas is risky due to poison-shooting mobs and "Chaos Clap" mechanics that can instantly deplete a Priest's health bar.

### Recommended Leveling Milestones

1.  **Level 1-5:** Focus on clearing local quests and learning your "Spark" mechanics.
2.  **Level 6-8:** Begin searching for "Critters" (pets) using a net. Pets provide minor passive buffs that assist in solo play.
3.  **Level 10:** This is the "no turning back" point. Before entering the level 10 dungeon, ensure your gear is upgraded using the weapon upgrade system, which increases attributes and grants access to unique artifacts.

## Mounts and Gold Management

Gold is a precious resource in Farever. You will need it for everything from buying "Black Pages" to recycling old items at the Spark Recycle station. One of the biggest goals for any player is obtaining a mount.

> **💡 Tip:** Save your gold early. A standard mount from the Mount Lady costs approximately **2,000 gold**. Avoid spending all your currency on low-level gear upgrades that you will eventually recycle.

If you find yourself short on funds, use the Bank located in the first main city to store valuable materials. Selling excess crafting components to other players or specialized merchants is the most reliable way to reach that 2,000 gold milestone by 2026.

## Gear Upgrades and Artifacts

The weapon system in Farever is unique. You can spend points to upgrade a weapon's attributes, but many players hesitate, fearing they will find a better weapon shortly after. For Priests, it is generally recommended to hold off on heavy upgrades until you find a "Blue" (Rare) quality staff.

### Crafting Checklist for Priests

| Item | Required Materials | Bonus Stat |
|---|---|---|
| **Scholar's Belt** | 1x Black Page, 2x Bright Powder | +2 Intellect |
| **Acolyte's Robe** | 3x Copper, 1x Purified Powder | +3 Spirit |
| **Light-Bender Staff** | 5x Copper, 2x Black Page | Unique Artifact Slot |

When you unlock a unique artifact, your Priest gains access to specialized abilities that can stun enemies or provide massive AoE (Area of Effect) heals. This is crucial when facing "blue mobs" that can otherwise one-shot unprepared players.

## Advanced Combat Tips

*   **Radius Awareness:** Many boss abilities have a visible radius. In 2026, server latency can sometimes make you appear inside a zone even if you think you are outside. Always give yourself extra space when a boss starts charging a "Chaos" ability.
*   **Recycling vs. Selling:** If you are desperate for gold for a mount, sell your items. If you need materials for the Enchanter job, use the Spark Recycle station to break down old gear.
*   **Pet Utility:** Don't ignore the "Critter" system. Even a basic pet can distract a mob for 2-3 seconds, giving you enough time to cast a crucial heal.

## FAQ

**Q: What is the best job for a Priest in Farever?**

A: The Enchanter is generally considered the best choice because it allows you to craft items with Intellect bonuses, such as the Scholar's Belt. However, if your group already has an Enchanter, picking Alchemist for potion brewing is a great alternative.

**Q: How much does a mount cost, and where do I get one?**

A: A mount costs 2,000 gold. You can purchase one from the Mount Lady located in the main hub cities. It is recommended to start saving your gold from level 1 to afford this by the time you reach the mid-game.

**Q: Should I upgrade my starting weapon?**

A: According to this **farever priest guide**, you should be cautious. While upgrading increases attributes, it costs points that might be better spent on a Rare (blue) weapon later. Only upgrade your starter weapon if you are struggling to clear level 5-6 mobs.

**Q: What should I do if I keep dying in the level 10 dungeon?**

A: Ensure you are not pulling multiple "blue mobs" at once. Priests have high utility but low physical defense. Use stuns and freeze mechanics if available, and always stay behind your party's tank or melee fighters to avoid poison projectiles.
