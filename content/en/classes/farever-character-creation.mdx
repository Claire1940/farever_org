## Selecting Your Class: The Foundation of Your Legend

Before you dive into the aesthetic details, you must choose a class. In Farever, your class dictates your resource management, combat flow, and utility within a group. Each of the four available classes offers a distinct "signature" mechanic that separates it from standard fantasy tropes.

### Warrior: The Relentless Juggernaut
Warriors are the masters of close-quarters combat. They rely on **Rage**, a resource generated by dealing and taking damage. This rage can be spent on devastating physical attacks or defensive buffs that allow them to thrive in the thick of the fight.

*   **Raging Smash:** Consumes rage to strike the ground, dealing 150% physical damage and knocking back enemies.
*   **Charge:** A mobility tool that stuns enemies for 1 second.
*   **Ignore Pain:** A vital defensive skill that reduces all incoming damage by 40% for 12 seconds.

### Rogue: The Swift Executioner
If you prefer precision over brute force, the Rogue utilizes **Combo Points**. By weaving together different attacks and skills, Rogues build momentum to unleash powerful finishing moves.

*   **Finisher:** A dashing strike that deals massive damage, increased by 45% for every combo point consumed.
*   **Shadow Step:** Instantly teleports the Rogue behind a target, providing a 2-second stun.
*   **Smoke Bomb:** A utility tool that allows allies within the cloud to dodge all physical attacks.

### Mage: The Spark Master
Mages in Farever use a unique **Spark** system. Unlike traditional mana-based casters, Mages must stay above a "flow threshold" to trigger enhanced effects. This rewards players who can maintain a constant rhythm of spellcasting.

*   **Spark Beam:** A channeled ray dealing 200% magic damage while regenerating Spark.
*   **Blink:** A classic teleportation move for repositioning.
*   **Mystic Empowerment:** Buffs the magic mastery of the entire party by 20%.

### Priest: The Divine Invoker
Priests channel power through **Prayers**. These are timed invocations that build over time and can be released for a variety of holy effects, ranging from massive heals to radiant crowd control.

*   **Judgment:** A nova that activates all currently "ready" prayers.
*   **Faithful Winds:** Increases ally movement speed by 70% for a short duration.
*   **Radiant Verdict:** Deals magic damage and stuns nearby enemies for 3 seconds.

## The Farever Character Creation Visual Suite

Once your class is set, the **farever character creation** system moves into visual aesthetics. The game utilizes three primary body types rather than rigid gender binary selections. This allows for a more inclusive approach where players can choose a muscular masculine frame, a feminine frame with distinct features, or a standard androgynous frame.

### Body Types and Skin Tones
The skin tone selection is impressively broad, featuring a wide range of warm and cool tones. Whether you want a pale, ethereal look for a Mage or a sun-kissed, rugged tone for a Warrior, the palette accommodates most preferences.

> **💡 Tip:** The lighting in the character creator is quite bright. If you are aiming for a specific skin tone, remember that it may look slightly darker or more saturated in the shadowed dungeons of the open world.

### Hair Styles and Colors
The hair texture in Farever is simplified but satisfying, fitting the vibrant art style perfectly. There are over 22 base styles available, ranging from short crops and fades to long flowing locks, braids, and buns. Notably, most hairstyles include physics, giving them a slight "wobble" during movement.

## Defining Facial Features: Advanced Customization

The true depth of the **farever character creation** system lies in the facial menus. Because the game doesn't lock features to specific body types, you have full control over how masculine or feminine your character appears regardless of their frame.

### Face Shapes and Jaws
There are seven primary face shapes. These are the most important tools for defining your character's silhouette.
*   **Masculine Shapes:** Opt for the more squared-off jawlines (such as options 1 and 4) to create a rugged, chiseled look.
*   **Feminine Shapes:** Rounder or more delicate chin options (like 6 and 7) provide a softer, more traditional feminine appearance.

### The Eye Color Phenomenon
One of the most surprising aspects of the system is the inclusion of 45 different eye colors. While some appear to be slight variations of others, the sheer volume allows you to find the exact shade of "mystic violet" or "hellfire red" you desire. Because the character models are so vibrant, these eye colors are clearly visible even during standard gameplay, unlike in many other RPGs.

### Nose and Lip Adjustments
While there are no sliders for minute details, the preset selections for noses and lips are varied enough to cover different ethnicities and personality types. You can choose from delicate, small noses to larger, more pronounced "heroic" bridges. Similarly, lip thickness can be adjusted to add a touch of personality—thicker lips often help in creating a more feminine or expressive face.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Beards are currently the only form of "facial accessory." There is no dedicated makeup tab, though some eye shapes come with built-in eyeliner or thicker lashes to simulate a cosmetic look.

## FAQ

**Q: Can I change my appearance after the farever character creation process?**

A: Currently, your base body type and facial structure are permanent once you start the game. However, certain in-game NPCs may allow for minor changes like hair style and color as you progress through the main hub.

**Q: Are certain classes locked to specific body types?**

A: No. Every class—Warrior, Rogue, Mage, and Priest—is available for all three body types. There are no gameplay penalties or bonuses associated with your visual choices.

**Q: Why are there so many eye colors but fewer hair colors?**

A: The developers focused on "striking visuals." Since the eyes are a focal point of the character models in dialogue, they provided 45 options to ensure every player feels unique. Hair colors are more limited but cover all the essential natural and fantasy tones.

**Q: Is there an archer or ranger class available?**

A: As of the 2026 launch, the four core classes are Warrior, Rogue, Mage, and Priest. Elements of ranged combat are handled by the Mage, while the Rogue handles the agile, dexterity-based gameplay. Future updates may expand this roster.
