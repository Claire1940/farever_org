## The Sparkmaster System: Core Mage Mechanics

At the heart of the Mage experience is the Sparkmaster mechanic. Instead of a mana bar, Mages utilize a "Spark" meter. Every weapon skill and final combo attack consumes Spark, while specific class skills are required to regenerate it. The most critical element of this system is the threshold notch in the middle of your Spark bar.

When your Spark is above this threshold (the right half of the bar), using a weapon skill or finishing an auto-attack combo triggers your active Conduits. You begin with two Conduit slots, expanding to three at level 6. These function as passive **farever talents** that proc automatically, adding layers of damage or utility to every action you take.

| Conduit Name | Primary Effect | Max Potential | Best Use Case |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Shard** | Fires 1 magic shard (10 damage) | 3 slots = 3 shards | Maximum burst and single-target DPS |
| **Power** | Increases Magic Mastery by % | Stacks up to 20 times | Long boss encounters and sustained scaling |

> **💡 Tip:** A balanced setup of one Power conduit and two Shard conduits is often superior to stacking three of the same. This allows you to build a 10% magic damage buff (Magic Mastery) while still benefitting from free missile procs.

## Mage Class Skills and Leveling Progression

As you level up in 2026, you will unlock active class skills that provide the foundation for your combat rotation. These skills are independent of your weapon and serve as your primary tools for resource management and defense.

### Essential Early-Game Skills

1.  **Spark Beam:** This is your primary engine. It channels a powerful beam for 2 seconds, and each tick regenerates 12 Spark. You should weave this into your rotation whenever it is off cooldown to keep your Spark bar above the conduit threshold.
2.  **Spark Shield:** Your main defensive tool. It grants a shield equal to 40% of your maximum health. Since it scales with Vitality, building health directly increases your survivability. The shield lasts for 60 seconds but has a 30-second cooldown, allowing you to "double-stack" the protection before entering a difficult fight.
3.  **Blink:** The quintessential mobility tool. Use this to reposition out of physical hitboxes or to close the gap for a finisher.

### Level-Based Talent Unlocks

| Level | Skill Name | Description |
|---|---|---|
| **10** | **Mystic Empowerment** | A 2-minute cooldown granting **30% CDR** for 15 seconds. |
| **15** | **Static Nova** | A large AOE blast that immobilizes enemies in a "bomb route." |
| **20** | **Chrono Reset** | Fully regenerates Spark and resets all weapon skill cooldowns. |
| **30** | **Overload** | Triggers one random conduit every second for 9 seconds. |

## Customizing Spells with Runes

Every active class skill in Farever can be modified using Runes. These items drop throughout the world and must be manually slotted into your skill tree to take effect. Runes allow you to tailor your **farever talents** to specific combat scenarios.

For example, Spark Beam can be upgraded with:
*   **Lightning Infusion:** Converts the beam into AOE damage (35% splash).
*   **Healing Infusion:** Heals the caster for 25% of the damage dealt.
*   **Frost Infusion:** Adds a slow effect to targets hit.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Simply "using" a Rune item in your inventory is not enough. You must open your skill tree and click the specific Rune so an opaque box appears around it, indicating it is active.

## Weapon Synergies and The Arsenal System

At level 7, you unlock the Arsenal slot. This is a game-changer for your **farever talents** because it allows you to equip a second weapon and use one of its skills without switching your main-hand weapon. This creates hybrid opportunities, such as using a Fire Staff for primary damage while pulling out Water Claws for a quick defensive uppercut.

### Starter Weapon Comparison

| Weapon | Type | Key Passive | Focus |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Apprentice's Grimoire** | Green (Basic) | None | Simple Fireball/Burn rotations |
| **Radiance (Fire Staff)** | Blue (Rare) | **Flame Bomb** | High AOE, Critical Strike synergies |
| **Ghost Clams** | Blue (Rare) | **Power of the Tides** | Support, Healing, and Crowd Control |

### The "Radiance" Burn Build
The Radiance staff is currently one of the most powerful options due to the **Flame Bomb** passive. Any non-DOT magic damage applies stacks of Flame Bomb; at 10 stacks, the target explodes. 

The real power comes from upgrading **Blazing Beam** (the flamethrower). Once upgraded to always critically strike, and combined with the passive that makes critical hits apply Flame Bomb, the flamethrower can trigger multiple explosions in a single channel. This synergy makes it the premier choice for clearing groups of enemies.

### The "Ghost Clams" Support Build
If you prefer a more methodical, durable playstyle, the Ghost Clams (halos) allow you to play as a "water bender." This weapon focuses on **Tidal Totems** that provide passive healing and explode for AOE damage whenever you use another skill. By stacking totems and using the **Riptide** ability, you can maintain high health while slowing enemies by 30% or more.

## Advanced Combat Tactics

Success in Farever requires mastering the soulslike elements of the game. Because everything has physical hitboxes, simply standing still and casting will result in a quick death.

1.  **Perfect Blocking:** Timing your block creates a parry. This regenerates your block gauge (stamina) faster, allowing you to stay in the pocket against aggressive melee enemies.
2.  **Auto-Attack Finishers:** Do not ignore your left-click. The final hit of an auto-attack combo (like the Fireball at the end of the Grimoire chain) consumes Spark and procs your conduits. If your Spark is full, weaving auto-attacks is a highly efficient way to deal damage.
3.  **The Burn Opener:** Always start fights with a high-impact spell like Pyroball. This applies a DOT effect and often grants your subsequent attacks a 25% increased critical strike chance.

## FAQ

**Q: How do I choose the best farever talents for a solo player?**

A: Solo players should prioritize a mix of the **Spark Shield** (with the Resurgence rune) and the **Radiance** staff. The shield provides the necessary survivability to handle mistakes, while the high AOE damage of the staff ensures you aren't overwhelmed by packs of mobs.

**Q: Can I change my conduits at any time?**

A: Yes, you can drag and drop your Shard and Power conduits into your Sparkmaster slots whenever you are out of combat. This allows you to swap to a high-Power build for bosses and a high-Shard build for general exploration.

**Q: What is the benefit of the Arsenal slot?**

A: The Arsenal slot allows you to "borrow" a skill from a weapon you aren't currently holding. For example, you can equip the Ghost Clams in your Arsenal to use a healing totem while your main hand holds a high-damage Fire Staff.

**Q: How does the "Overload" talent work at level 30?**

A: Overload is your ultimate ability. For 9 seconds, it bypasses the Spark requirement and automatically triggers one of your equipped conduits every second. This results in nine free procs of Shards or Power buffs on top of your regular spell rotation.
