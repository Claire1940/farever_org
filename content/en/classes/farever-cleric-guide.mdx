Choosing a support role in a high-stakes action RPG requires a balance of survivability and utility. This **farever cleric guide** is designed to take you from a novice acolyte to a master of divine intervention. Unlike traditional MMOs where healers stand in the back, this **farever cleric guide** focuses on the game's unique "soulslike" combat, where positioning and timing are just as important as your healing throughput. In Farever, Clerics don't just mend wounds; they engage in the thick of battle, utilizing physical hitboxes and active mitigation to protect their allies while dealing significant holy damage.

## Core Combat Mechanics for Clerics

To succeed as a Cleric, you must first master the fundamental combat systems of Farever. The game utilizes a stamina-based defensive system rather than a traditional mana pool. For the Cleric, this means managing your cooldowns and block gauge is the difference between life and death.

### Defensive Maneuvers
Every Cleric starts with a basic block and a dodge. Your dodge typically has two charges that operate on a short cooldown. To the left of your action bar, you will see the block gauge. Initiating a block consumes a portion of this gauge. However, if you time your block perfectly against an incoming attack, you perform a **Parry**. A successful parry not only staggers many enemies but also causes your block gauge to regenerate significantly faster.

### The Resource System: Divine Fervor
Farever removes the frustration of mana management. Instead, Clerics operate on a resource called **Fervor**. Your weapon skills and final combo attacks consume Fervor to trigger powerful secondary effects. While Fervor regenerates slowly over time, you must use specific "Generator" skills to keep your meter high during intense boss encounters.

| Mechanic | Function | Cleric Benefit |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Dodge** | I-frame movement | Essential for avoiding heavy unblockable hits. |
| **Block Gauge** | Damage mitigation | Allows Clerics to stay in melee range to apply buffs. |
| **Perfect Parry** | Stagger & Regen | Refills the block gauge and opens windows for heals. |
| **Fervor Meter** | Skill Resource | Powers your "Benedictions" and high-tier holy spells. |

## The Cleric Passive: Divine Favor

Every class in Farever features a unique passive mechanic. For the Cleric, this is **Divine Favor**. This system allows you to slot "Blessings" (similar to the Mage's conduits) that activate whenever you consume Fervor above a specific threshold.

You will see a notch in the middle of your Fervor bar. If your meter is above 50%, using a weapon skill or a final combo attack will proc your slotted Blessings. You start with two slots and unlock a third around level 6.

### Recommended Blessing Setups
*   **Radiant Shard:** Fires a bolt of holy energy at the target. Great for consistent DPS.
*   **Hallowed Ground:** Creates a small patch of healing light at your feet.
*   **Divine Might:** Increases your Physical and Holy mastery by a percentage for 15 seconds, stacking up to 20 times.

> **💡 Tip:** For solo leveling, run two Radiant Shards and one Divine Might. This ensures you have enough damage to clear mobs while maintaining a scaling damage buff.

## Essential Cleric Weapons

At level 5, you will receive a mystery cache allowing you to choose your first rare (blue) weapon. Your choice here will define your playstyle for the next several levels.

### The Arbiter’s Mace (Holy/Physical)
This is the standard "Battle Cleric" choice. It focuses on heavy physical hits that apply a "Sanctified" debuff to enemies. When you hit a Sanctified enemy with a final combo attack, it triggers an explosion of holy light that heals nearby allies.

### Relics of the Martyr (Pure Support)
Relics are ranged weapons that allow you to tether to allies. While tethered, a portion of the damage you deal is converted into healing for the linked target. This is the premier choice for group play and dungeon crawling.

### Arsenal System at Level 7
Upon reaching level 7, you unlock the **Arsenal slot**. This allows you to equip a secondary weapon and use one of its specific abilities without switching your entire hotbar. For example, a Cleric using a Mace can equip a Relic in the Arsenal slot to access a quick emergency heal while maintaining their melee presence.

| Weapon Type | Primary Stat | Best Use Case |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Maces** | Strength / Holy | Solo leveling and frontline tanking. |
| **Relics** | Intellect / Holy | Dedicated healing and group utility. |
| **Shields** | Vitality | Maximum survivability and parry windows. |

## Skills and Skill Runes

As you progress toward level 30, you will find **Skill Runes**. These items drop from bosses and world events, allowing you to modify your core class skills. You must manually click these in your skill tree to activate them.

### Key Cleric Skills
1.  **Holy Beam (Level 1):** Your primary Fervor generator. It channels a beam that deals damage and restores Fervor with every tick.
2.  **Aegis of Light (Level 4):** Provides a shield equal to 40% of your max health. This is your primary defensive tool.
3.  **Benediction (Level 10):** A 2-minute cooldown that reduces all skill cooldowns by 30% for 15 seconds.
4.  **Resurrection (Level 20):** Allows you to revive a fallen ally in combat. This has a long 5-minute cooldown.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Always keep your Aegis of Light active before engaging a boss. The shield lasts for 60 seconds, but the cooldown is only 30 seconds, allowing you to "double-stack" the protection if timed correctly.

## Progression and Leveling Tips

Leveling a Cleric in Farever requires a mix of aggressive combat and tactical retreating. Because you lack a traditional mana pool, you are never truly "out of the fight," but you can find yourself "Fervor-starved" if you don't weave auto-attacks between your spells.

1.  **Master the Final Combo:** Your fourth auto-attack hit is the "Final Combo Attack." This is what triggers your Blessings. Never cancel your auto-attack chain early if you can help it.
2.  **Upgrade Weapons Early:** Weapons in Farever have their own levels. As you use a weapon, you gain ranks that can be spent to enhance its specific skills. A Rank 3 Mace is significantly more powerful than a Rank 1 Mace of a higher rarity.
3.  **Utilize the Environment:** Since everything has physical hitboxes, you can use terrain to block line-of-sight from ranged attackers while your Holy Beam channels.

## FAQ

**Q: Can a Cleric be a viable solo class in Farever?**

A: Absolutely. By focusing on Mace weapons and the Divine Might blessing, the **farever cleric guide** build provides enough self-healing and damage reduction to tackle almost all world content solo.

**Q: How do I get more Skill Runes?**

A: Skill Runes are rare drops from Dungeon Bosses and Elite mobs in Prime Valley. They can also be purchased from specific Reputation Vendors once you reach "Honored" status with the Acolytes of Light.

**Q: Does the Cleric use Mana?**

A: No. Like all classes in Farever, the Cleric uses a cooldown-based system supplemented by a class-specific resource (Fervor). You never have to worry about drinking water or mana potions between pulls.

**Q: What is the most important stat for a Cleric?**

A: For a balanced build, focus on **Holy Mastery** to increase your healing and spell damage, and **Vitality** to increase the strength of your Aegis of Light shield.
