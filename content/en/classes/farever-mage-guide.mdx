## Combat Fundamentals: Soulslike Sorcery

Before diving into complex rotations, you must master the physical nature of combat. Every enemy attack has a distinct hitbox; if you aren't there when the swing lands, you won't take damage. This makes the Mage a highly mobile class that relies on active defense rather than just standing still and casting.

*   **Blocking and Parrying:** You possess a block gauge (stamina). Blocking a hit consumes a chunk of this gauge, which then regenerates. However, timing your block perfectly results in a **Parry**, which causes the gauge to regenerate significantly faster and can stagger opponents.
*   **Dodging:** Your dash has two charges on independent cooldowns. Use these to escape AOE (Area of Effect) circles or reposition for a better Spark Beam angle.
*   **Auto-Attack Finishers:** Every weapon has a combo chain. The final hit of your auto-attack sequence is the "Final Combo Attack," which plays a massive role in your resource management.

> **💡 Tip:** Always keep an eye on your block gauge. If it breaks, you'll be stunned, leaving you vulnerable to high-damage physical combos.

## Understanding the Sparkmaster Passive

The heart of the Mage class is the **Sparkmaster** mechanic. Your resource is called **Spark**, represented by a blue bar above your hotbar. While most classes manage mana, the Mage manages the consumption and generation of Spark to trigger powerful auxiliary effects called Conduits.

### The Spark Threshold
There is a notch in the middle of your Spark bar. If your meter is above this 50% threshold, using any weapon skill or a final combo attack will consume Spark to activate your selected **Conduits**. If you fall below the threshold, your weapon skills still work, but you lose the massive bonus damage and utility from your conduits.

### Conduit Configurations
You start with two conduit slots, expanding to three as you level up. You can mix and match these to fit your playstyle:

| Conduit Type | Effect | Best Use Case |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Shard** | Fires a magic missile dealing 10 magic damage. | Maximum burst and single-target DPS. |
| **Power** | Increases Magic Mastery by a % for 15s (Stacks up to 20x). | Long boss fights and sustained AOE scaling. |

**Recommended Setup:** For general leveling, running **one Power conduit and two Shard conduits** is optimal. This allows you to stack up to a 10% total magic damage increase while still proccing two free magic missiles with every skill cast.

## Essential Mage Class Skills

While your weapons provide your primary attacks, your class skills provide the utility, defense, and resource generation needed to survive.

### 1. Spark Beam (The Generator)
This is your most important skill. It channels a beam that deals magic damage over 2 seconds, but more importantly, each tick regenerates 12 Spark. You should weave Spark Beam into your rotation whenever your meter drops near the 50% threshold.

### 2. Spark Shield
A vital defensive tool that grants a shield equal to 40% of your max health. It has a 30-second cooldown, but the shield lasts for a full minute. 
> **Warning:** Spark Shield also consumes Spark. Ensure you are above the threshold before casting if you want to proc your conduits for extra defensive utility.

### 3. High-Level Unlocks (Levels 10-30)
As you progress through 2026, you will unlock powerful cooldowns that define the late-game Mage experience:
*   **Mystic Empowerment (Lvl 10):** Grants 30% Cooldown Reduction (CDR) for 15 seconds.
*   **Static Nova (Lvl 15):** A massive AOE blast that immobilizes enemies.
*   **Chrono Reset (Lvl 20):** Fully regenerates Spark and resets all weapon skill cooldowns.
*   **Overload (Lvl 30):** Triggers one free random conduit every second for 9 seconds.

## Weapon Archetypes and Synergies

In Farever, weapons aren't just stat sticks; they define your playstyle. Blue-tier weapons and above feature unique passives that can be upgraded as the weapon levels up.

### Radiant Staff (Fire/AOE)
The Radiant Staff is the premier choice for players who want to "blast." Its passive, **Flame Bomb**, applies stacks to enemies whenever you deal non-DOT magic damage or land a critical strike. At 10 stacks (when upgraded), the bomb explodes for massive AOE damage.

*   **Blazing Beam:** A flamethrower that can be upgraded to always critically strike. This synergy allows you to proc Flame Bomb explosions multiple times in a single channel.
*   **Pyrolasm:** An AOE burst that increases all magic damage dealt to the target by 8%.

### Ghost Clams of the Low Tide (Water/Support)
If you prefer a more tactical or supportive role, the Ghost Clams utilize **Tidal Totems**.
*   **Healing:** Standing near your totem provides passive health regeneration.
*   **Power of the Tides:** Using any skill causes your totems to erupt in a water burst. 
*   **Crowd Control:** Upgrades allow your water skills to slow enemies, making you an excellent kiter.

## The Arsenal System: Hybrid Building

Unlocked at level 7, the **Arsenal System** allows you to equip a secondary weapon and use one of its skills while keeping your main weapon's auto-attacks and passives. This is where the **farever mage guide** strategies become truly advanced.

For example, you can equip the **Radiant Staff** as your primary weapon for its explosive damage but slot the **Ghost Clams' Tidal Totem** into your Arsenal slot. This gives your high-damage fire mage a source of consistent passive healing and a way to proc "Power of the Tides" explosions using your fire spells.

To set this up:
1.  Open your character menu and drag the secondary weapon to the Arsenal slot.
2.  Control + Left Click the weapon to select which specific skill you want to use.
3.  Note that weapon upgrades (ranks) carry over to the Arsenal slot, so keep your secondary weapons leveled!

## Rune Customization

Every active class skill has three Rune slots. These are items found in the world that fundamentally change how a spell functions. You must manually click the Rune in your skill tree to activate it (indicated by an opaque box).

*   **Spark Beam Runes:** Can add Lightning Infusion (AOE damage) or Life Infusion (Heals for 25% of damage dealt).
*   **Spark Shield Runes:** The Resurgence Rune restores Spark to you when the shield is destroyed, creating a "mana-positive" defensive loop.

## FAQ

**Q: Is there a way to increase my maximum Spark?**

A: While the base Spark gauge is fixed, you can improve your "Spark Economy" by using Runes that refund Spark or by using the Spark Beam skill more frequently in your rotation. High-level gear also provides "Magic Mastery," which makes each Spark-consuming cast more impactful.

**Q: Can I play a melee Mage in Farever?**

A: Yes! Weapons like the "Claudius" fist weapons allow for a melee-focused water mage playstyle. These weapons focus on close-quarters combat while still utilizing the Sparkmaster passive to proc magic shards and power buffs.

**Q: Which conduit is best for leveling?**

A: As mentioned in this **farever mage guide**, a mix of Shard and Power is best. Shards help you clear individual mobs faster, while Power ensures you have the damage scaling needed to take down elite enemies and bosses in the Prime Valley.

**Q: How do I unlock more Arsenal slots?**

A: Currently, you unlock your first Arsenal slot at level 7. Future updates in 2026 may expand this system, but for now, you must choose your one cross-weapon skill carefully to complement your main-hand weapon's weaknesses.
