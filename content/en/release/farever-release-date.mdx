## Understanding the Class and Weapon Systems

One of the standout features of Farever is its flexible class system. While you start by choosing one of four base classes, your actual combat utility is heavily dictated by the weapons you choose to wield. This "soft-class" approach allows for a high degree of customization, enabling players to mix and match traditional roles with unexpected weaponry.

### Starting Classes Overview

| Class | Primary Role | Core Attribute | Playstyle |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Warrior** | Tank/Brawler | Physical Strength | Frontline engagement and crowd control. |
| **Priest** | Support/Healer | Divine Magic | Healing allies and providing defensive buffs. |
| **Rogue** | DPS/Assassin | Agility | High burst damage and poison application. |
| **Mage** | Ranged DPS | Arcane Intellect | Area of effect (AOE) spells and elemental control. |

### The Arsenal and Mastery Systems

At level 7, players unlock the **Arsenal System**, which is a game-changer for build diversity. This system allows you to equip a secondary weapon. While you do not swap between weapons in the middle of a combo, you gain the ability to slot one skill from your secondary weapon into your main skill bar. For example, a Priest might equip a sword and shield as their secondary "Arsenal" to gain a defensive passive or a specific melee strike, effectively creating a Paladin-style build.

Furthermore, as you defeat enemies, you may find **Masteries** on the ground. These are consumable items that add slight variations to your existing skills. This adds a layer of RNG-based progression that keeps combat feeling fresh as you hunt for the perfect skill modifiers.

## Leveling and Progression Mechanics

Leveling in Farever differs from traditional "grind-heavy" MMOs. While you do get experience from killing mobs, the most efficient way to level up is by engaging with the **Codex System** and world exploration.

### How to Gain XP Efficiently

1.  **Filling the Codex:** For every monster type in the game, you need to defeat a specific number (usually eight) to complete a Codex entry. Completing an entry provides a significant XP boost.
2.  **World Chests:** Scattered throughout the open world are chests. Some can be kicked open immediately, while others require completing a mini-quest or surviving an ambush. These chests provide both loot and experience.
3.  **Instance Dungeons:** These are designed for up to four players. While they provide great loot, the primary XP gain comes from the first-time completion and the associated Codex entries for the bosses within.
4.  **Quests:** Simple quests are found near towers. These usually involve hunting specific mini-bosses or gathering materials.

### Progression Milestones

| Level | Unlockable Feature | Benefit |
|---|---|---|
| **Level 1** | Base Class Skills | Initial combat abilities and weapon access. |
| **Level 7** | **Arsenal System** | Ability to use a skill from a secondary weapon. |
| **Level 10** | **Talent Tree** | Unlock specialized passives (e.g., Poison chance for Rogues). |
| **Level 13** | Level Cap (Current) | Maximum character level for the initial Early Access build. |

> **⚠️ Warning:** Experience in a party is split between members. While grouping makes filling the Codex faster, it does not provide a raw XP multiplier, so ensure your party stays together to maximize efficiency.

## Exploration and Life Skills

The world of Farever is divided into regions that are unlocked by visiting **Towers**. These towers act as fast-travel points and reveal local quests on your map. Traversal is made easier once you save up **2,000 Gold** to purchase your first mount, a task that generally takes a few hours of active play.

### Crafting and Gathering

Crafting in Farever is straightforward and does not require separate leveling for gathering. If you see a resource node, you can mine or collect it immediately. The crafting professions include:

*   **Blacksmithing:** Forging heavy armor and melee weapons.
*   **Leatherworking:** Creating medium armor using hides dropped by mobs.
*   **Tailoring:** Crafting light armor and staves.
*   **Alchemy:** Brewing potions for health and mana restoration.

The crafting system uses a talent-tree style progression. As you craft items, you unlock further nodes in the tree, allowing you to create higher-tier gear.

## Performance and Optimization Tips

As an indie title launching in 2026, Farever faces some optimization hurdles. The developer has implemented several fixes for "Memory Link" issues and server desync, but players should still take precautions to ensure a smooth experience.

*   **Lower Mob Density Areas:** If you experience stuttering, move away from high-density mob spawns or crowded starter islands.
*   **Adjust Shadow Quality:** Reducing shadow and lighting effects provides the most significant FPS boost in the current build.
*   **SSD Installation:** Due to the way the game streams assets in the open world, installing Farever on an SSD is highly recommended to reduce hitching during traversal.
