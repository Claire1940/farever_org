## The Weapon Skill System (Arsenal)

One of the most interesting features revealed during the **farever beta access** playtests is the "Arsenal" system. Much like Guild Wars 2, your active skills are determined by the weapon you currently have equipped. For example, a Warrior using a Sword and Shield will have defensive capabilities, but switching to Dual Axes completely changes the hotbar to focus on high-speed AoE (Area of Effect) attacks.

As you use these skills, they rank up individually. Even if your character's main level stays the same for a period, your "Practice Strike" or "Shield Bash" can level up, increasing their damage output or reducing their cooldowns. This adds a layer of horizontal progression that allows players to specialize in specific weapon types.

## Professions and Gathering

Farever isn't just about combat; it features a robust set of professions that are essential for high-level progression. In the current build, players can visit trainers in major towns to learn a "Job." However, there is a catch: you can typically only focus on one primary profession at a time, encouraging player-to-player trading and guild cooperation.

| Profession | Primary Function | Key Materials |

| **Alchemy** | Creates potions, elixirs, and combat buffs. | Herbs, Monster Essences. |

> **💡 Tip:** Check the second town early in your journey. The crafting trainers there provide the necessary tools and initial quests to jumpstart your professional career.

## Exploration and World Events

The world of Farever is divided into zones that players can "unlock" by visiting specific teleport spots or vantage points. Once a zone is unlocked, the map UI reveals various quests, points of interest, and "Codex" entries.

The Codex is a vital tool for any player with **farever beta access**. It tracks every mob you kill, showing their potential loot drops and crafting material yields. Completing Codex entries for specific regions often rewards the player with experience or unique equipment.

### Elite Camps and Dungeons
Throughout the overworld, you will encounter Elite Camps. These are indicated by "chunky" NPCs that are significantly tankier than standard mobs. Clearing these camps often rewards players with a treasure chest containing blue-tier (Rare) gear. 

For those seeking a greater challenge, the game features instanced dungeons:
