## Understanding the Dungeon Structure

In Farever, dungeons are set instance locations found within each major region. Unlike the sprawling open world where you might encounter hundreds of other players, these instances are focused experiences designed to test your combat prowess and puzzle-solving skills. The first dungeon is located in the Meridian region and acts as a gateway to the game's deeper progression systems.

The layout is bespoke, meaning it isn't procedurally generated. You will encounter a mix of basic enemies, elite "shiny" mobs, and complex traversal sections. Before entering, ensure your "Arsenal" is properly configured, as you cannot easily swap your sub-weapon skills once the fighting begins.

## Preparing Your Build for the First Run

Before you tackle the **farever first dungeon**, you must choose a class that fits your playstyle. While Farever allows for significant customization through weapons, your base class (Warrior, Cleric, Rogue, or Mage) provides the foundation for your active and passive skills.

> **💡 Tip:** Even if you are playing a damage-focused class like the Mage, consider equipping a sub-weapon in your Arsenal that provides a stun or defensive utility. Stuns are invaluable for interrupting elite enemy casts.

At level 7, you unlock the Arsenal slot. This allows you to take a second weapon and "borrow" one of its active skills. For your first dungeon run, a Shield Bash from the Warrior's kit or a Blink from the Mage can provide the mobility and crowd control needed to survive the final boss.

## Navigating Traversal Puzzles

Farever places a heavy emphasis on movement. The **farever first dungeon** features several sections where combat is secondary to your ability to navigate the environment. You will need to utilize your glider, grapple hook, and launch pads to reach the boss arena.

1.  **Launch Pads:** These propel you vertically or horizontally. Always look for the next pad before jumping, as missing one can lead to a lethal fall.
2.  **Grapple Points:** Use these to yo-yo across gaps. Remember that you can maintain momentum by releasing the grapple at the apex of your swing.
3.  **Glider Usage:** Unlike other MMOs, Farever does not have a stamina meter for gliding. You can stay airborne as long as you have height, which is essential for certain "orb collection" puzzles within the dungeon.

## Boss Strategy: The First Guardian

The climax of the **farever first dungeon** is a multi-phase boss fight that requires strict mechanical execution. This boss utilizes area-of-effect (AoE) fire attacks that persist on the ground, limiting your movement over time.

### Phase 1: Positioning
The boss will periodically target you with fire pools. It is vital to kite the boss to the edges or corners of the arena when these pools drop. If you leave the fire in the center, you will have nowhere to dodge during the later, more chaotic phases.

### Phase 2: The Charge and Perfect Block
Once the boss reaches 60% health, it will begin a charging attack. You can see a red glint indicating an unblockable move, but standard physical strikes can be "Perfect Blocked." A perfect block allows you to continue your attack chain without interruption, which is the key to maximizing your DPS (Damage Per Second).

### Phase 3: Enrage
In the final 20%, the boss increases its attack speed. Use your "Rune" abilities here—specifically those that reduce incoming damage or increase your lifesteal on critical hits. If you have been leveling your weapons at the blacksmith, your tier 3 upgrades (like increased crit chance) will shine here.

## Advanced Tips for Efficient Runs

Once you have cleared the dungeon once, you may want to farm it for specific weapon drops or experience points. To optimize your runs, focus on the following:

*   **Skill Expression:** Practice the "1-2-Block-3-4" combo. By blocking in the middle of your basic attack chain, you can mitigate damage without resetting your combo finisher, which usually deals 200% more damage.
*   **Job Leveling:** Ensure you have the Blacksmithing job active. Upgrading your weapons to Tier 3 adds passive effects like "2% increased crit," which scales exponentially with fast-attacking weapons like dual Berserker axes.
*   **Inventory Management:** Since Farever lacks an auto-sort feature in early 2026, keep your bags clear of "trash" loot (like crab shells) before entering the dungeon to ensure you have space for all the blue drops.

## FAQ

**Q: What level should I be for the farever first dungeon?**

A: It is recommended to be at least level 7 to 10. While you can enter earlier, having access to the Arsenal system at level 7 significantly increases your survivability and damage output.

**Q: Can I solo the first dungeon?**

A: Yes, the dungeon scales based on the number of players. However, bringing a Cleric or a player with healing totems makes the final boss much more manageable if you haven't mastered perfect blocking yet.

**Q: How many times can I run the dungeon?**

A: There is currently no daily limit on dungeon entries in Farever. You can farm the instance as many times as you like to roll for better stats on your blue gear or to gather crafting components.

**Q: Where do I find the entrance to the Meridian dungeon?**

A: Look for the dungeon icon on your regional activity list. It is usually located near the center of the Meridian zone, marked by a large stone gateway with a blue shimmering portal.
