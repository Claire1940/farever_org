## Quest Prerequisites and Activation

Before you can embark on the **farever gotta catch one quest**, your character must meet specific criteria within the world of Farever. Unlike standard side missions, this quest is tethered to the main storyline progression, meaning you cannot simply stumble upon it without some prior legwork.

To trigger the mission, you must reach Level 10 and have completed the "Introduction to Taming" tutorial in the capital city of Aethelgard. Once these conditions are met, head to the Whispering Woods and locate Elder Thorne near the Great Oak. He will task you with the mission, emphasizing that "to understand the bond, one must first master the hunt."

| Requirement | Type | Description |

> **💡 Tip:** Ensure your inventory is clear of junk items before speaking to Thorne. The rewards for this quest require multiple slots, and you wouldn't want to leave rare loot behind.

## Locating the Target: The Spectral Glider

The core objective of the **farever gotta catch one quest** is to find and capture a Spectral Glider. This creature is notoriously difficult to track because it only appears during specific weather patterns and in hidden clearings within the woods.

The Spectral Glider prefers "Luminous Fog" conditions, which occur randomly every 20 minutes of real-world time. You can check the current weather forecast by looking at the sundial in your UI. Once the fog rolls in, head to the Northern Ridge of the Whispering Woods. Listen for a high-pitched whistling sound—this is the Glider's unique call.

### Tracking Steps

1.  **Monitor the Weather:** Wait for the Luminous Fog state in the Whispering Woods.
2.  **Locate the Ridge:** Move to the coordinates **X:442, Y:891** on your map.
3.  **Use Scent Lures:** Deploy a Basic Herbivore Lure to increase the spawn rate.
4.  **Initiate Combat:** Once the Glider appears, engage it carefully. Do not use high-damage AOE spells as they may accidentally defeat the creature instead of weakening it.

## Capture Mechanics and Battle Strategy

Capturing monsters in Farever isn't just about lowering their health; it involves a "Rhythm Sync" system that requires precision. During the **farever gotta catch one quest**, the Spectral Glider will attempt to flee if its stamina isn't depleted alongside its health.

| Battle Phase | Action Required | Expected Outcome |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Phase 1: Weaken** | Use **Light Attacks** only | Reduce HP to below 25%. |
| **Phase 2: Daze** | Use a **Flash Powder** item | Stuns the Glider for 5 seconds. |
| **Phase 3: Deploy** | Use the **Silver Snare** | Initiates the capture mini-game. |
| **Phase 4: Sync** | Follow on-screen prompts | Secures the creature permanently. |

If you fail the capture mini-game, the Glider will teleport to a different part of the ridge. You have three attempts before the fog clears and you must wait for the next weather cycle. 

> **⚠️ Warning:** Do not use Poison or Burn status effects. If the Spectral Glider faints from damage-over-time effects, the quest will fail, and you will have to restart the encounter from the beginning.

## FAQ

**Q: Can I redo the farever gotta catch one quest if I get a low rank?**

A: Unfortunately, the quest is not repeatable on the same character profile. If you are aiming for the S-Rank rewards, it is highly recommended to save your game manually right before entering the Northern Ridge so you can reload if the capture doesn't go perfectly.

**Q: Where is Elder Thorne if he isn't at the Great Oak?**

A: If Thorne is missing, check your quest log to ensure you haven't accidentally triggered the "Invasion of Aethelgard" event. During this world event, Thorne moves to the Hidden Grotto for safety. He will return to the Great Oak once the event concludes.

**Q: Is the Spectral Glider good for late-game combat?**

A: While it is an early-game creature, the Spectral Glider has one of the highest Agility growth rates in the game. If you obtain the Epic Variant through an S-Rank completion of the quest, it can remain a viable support unit well into the mid-game, especially when evolved into the Phantom Harrier.

**Q: What happens if I lose the Silver Snare provided by the quest?**

A: If you lose or accidentally sell the Silver Snare, you can purchase a replacement from the "Quest Recovery" vendor in Aethelgard for 200 Gold. However, the quest must be active in your log for the item to appear in the vendor's inventory.
