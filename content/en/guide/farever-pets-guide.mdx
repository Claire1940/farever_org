## Understanding the Companion System

In FarEver, companions are more than just aesthetic additions; they are part of a broader collection system that adds depth to your character's journey. You can manage your pets by accessing your collections menu, which is bound to the "Y" key by default. This interface allows you to view your current stable, equip your favorite companions, and track which creatures you have yet to discover.

### Managing Your Pets and Collections

| Feature | Key Binding / Location | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| **Collections Menu** | Y | View and equip active companions |
| **Shop Interface** | Escape > Shop | Claim starter rewards and free items |
| **Guild Merchant** | Village Hub | Purchase capture nets and supplies |

> **💡 Pro Tip:** Always check the shop immediately upon starting your game. The developers often include a free companion in the initial item bundle, providing you with an instant boost to your collection.

## How to Catch Pets in the Wild

Beyond the pets you receive as initial gifts, the game features an immersive catching mechanic that allows you to expand your roster while exploring. To catch wild pets, you will need a specific tool known as a "Net."

1. **Visit the Guild Merchant:** Head to the main village hub and interact with the merchant.
2. **Purchase a Net:** Navigate to the "Trade" tab. Ensure you have enough gold saved from your early questing.
3. **Equip the Net:** Place the net into your active weapon slot. 
4. **Locate and Capture:** Once you find a creature in the wild, use the net to initiate the capture process.

**Important Note:** Because the net occupies an active weapon slot, it is highly recommended that you only attempt to catch pets when you are in a safe area or finished with combat encounters for the day. Attempting to catch a creature while wearing a net instead of a combat weapon will leave you vulnerable to enemy attacks.

## Optimizing Your Arsenal and Utility

While pets provide companionship, your combat effectiveness relies on your arsenal and skill management. As you level up, remember that you can equip multiple weapons to gain passive buffs and specific active skills.

### Weapon Utility Comparison

| Weapon Type | Primary Utility | Recommended For |
|---|---|---|
| **Scepter** | Magic Damage / Healing | Support and Ranged builds |
| **Sword** | Basic Attacks / DPS | Melee survivalists |
| **Net** | Creature Capture | Exploration and collection phases |

> **⚠️ Warning:** Never enter a high-level dungeon with a capture net equipped in your primary weapon slot. Always swap back to your combat-ready gear before engaging in boss fights or difficult map objectives.

## FAQ

**Q: Where do I find my first pet in FarEver?**

A: You can claim your first free pet by pressing the Escape key, navigating to the Shop menu, and collecting the free starter bundle. Once claimed, go to your Collections (default key Y) to equip it.

**Q: Do pets provide combat bonuses?**

A: In the current version of the game, pets are primarily companions that follow you around. Focus on upgrading your weapons, runes, and class skills for combat performance, while using pets to enhance your exploration experience.

**Q: Is it possible to lose my net after catching a pet?**

A: The net is a tool purchased from the guild merchant. While it occupies a weapon slot, it does not disappear after a single use, but you should always be mindful of your gear loadout when switching between exploration and combat.

**Q: What is the most efficient way to use this Farever pets guide?**

A: Use this guide to prioritize your early gold spending. By securing your inventory space and healing potions first, you will have more freedom to explore and purchase the nets needed to find every pet in the game.
