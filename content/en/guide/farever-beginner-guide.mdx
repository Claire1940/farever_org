## Early Game Mobility: Securing Your First Mount

One of the most common mistakes new players make is traversing the world on foot for far too long. Mobility is the backbone of efficiency in Farever. As soon as you gain control of your character, your first priority should be visiting the in-game shop. Currently, players can claim a free mount to significantly boost their travel speed.

Once you have collected the mount items from the shop, you must navigate to your collection menu to equip it. Simply owning the mount is not enough; it must be active in your equipment slot. After it is equipped, pressing the shift key allows you to move much faster across the terrain. 

| Mobility Method | Speed Increase | Requirement |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Walking/Running** | Base Speed | None |
| **Free Mount** | Significant Boost | Shop Claim & Collection Menu Equip |
| **Dashing** | Burst Speed | Stamina Cost |

> **💡 Tip:** Don't be the player running barefoot on the beach. Get your mount immediately to save hours of travel time during your initial questing phase.

## Combat Mechanics and Mastery

Combat in Farever is more than just clicking buttons; it requires a mix of timing, positioning, and resource management. Two of the most critical defensive tools at your disposal are dodging and perfect blocking.

### The Power of Perfect Blocks
While a standard block reduces incoming damage, a **Perfect Block** is a game-changer. Executing a block right before an enemy attack lands reduces the damage received to a minimum and significantly lowers the stamina consumed from your block meter. This is particularly vital during boss encounters where your dash might be on cooldown or your resources are depleted.

### Maintaining Combos with Dodging
A unique mechanic in Farever is the ability to maintain attack chains through movement. Most weapons have a multi-hit combo that ends in a powerful finisher. In many games, dodging would reset this sequence. However, in Farever, you can weave a dodge into your rotation without dropping the combo. For example, if you are using a Grimoire to cast fireballs, you can cast twice, dodge an incoming attack, and then cast the final fireball to trigger the finisher.

### Sparkling Monsters
While exploring, keep an eye out for "Sparkling Monsters." These are elite versions of standard mobs that offer superior rewards.
*   **Unique Gear:** Defeating them for the first time often drops high-tier equipment.
*   **Farming:** These monsters can be farmed for specific materials, making them a priority target for players looking to upgrade their gear early.

## Weapon Progression and Customization

Your weapons in Farever grow alongside your character. Every weapon, such as the Radiance or various Grimoires, features its own experience bar. As you use a weapon in combat, it levels up, unlocking new skills and upgrade paths.

### Leveling vs. Upgrading
It is important to distinguish between leveling a weapon and upgrading its rank. 
1.  **Leveling:** Done through combat. Unlocks skill points and rank-up opportunities.
2.  **Upgrading:** Done at upgrade stations in outposts. This requires specific materials like **Spark Dust**. Upgrading increases the "star" rating of the weapon, providing a massive boost to base stats.

### The Arsenal Slot and Runes
Upon reaching level 7, you unlock the **Arsenal Slot**. This allows you to equip a secondary weapon and utilize one of its specific skills, offering immense build freedom. Furthermore, you can find and equip **Runes** to modify your class skills. 

| Rune Effect Type | Example Benefit |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Lifesteal/Healing** | Heals for a % of damage dealt by the skill |
| **Mobility** | Adds a secondary teleport or "blink" back effect |
| **Damage Buff** | Increases the power of the next attack after skill use |

> **Warning:** Runes do not auto-equip. Once you unlock a rune, you must manually enter the skills menu and ensure the gray square is active on the skill you wish to modify.

## Inventory Management and Jobs

As a "loot goblin," your inventory will fill up rapidly. Managing your space is a core part of this **farever beginner guide**. You start with limited slots, but you can expand this by equipping pouches and bags.

### Expanding Your Pouch Slots
You can equip up to five bags or pouches in your inventory bar.
*   **Small Pouches:** Increase size by 6 slots. These can be purchased for 250 gold from trade merchants, but it is better to find them in treasure chests or as quest rewards.
*   **Linen Bags:** Increase size by 8 slots. These are crafted once you unlock the **Outfielder** job.

### The Banking System
When your bags are full, visit a Guild Merchant to access the bank. This allows you to deposit materials and gear you don't immediately need. While the current UI lacks an "auto-sort" button, using filters can help you manage your stored items more effectively.

### Unlocking Jobs
To learn a profession, speak to the NPC named **"Job Well Done."** 
*   **Cost Progression:** The first job is relatively cheap (around 200-400 gold), but the price increases significantly with every subsequent job you learn.
*   **Priority:** Choose a job that fits your immediate needs (like Outfielder for bags or a blacksmithing equivalent for gear) before spending gold on secondary professions.

## Exploration and Rewards

Farever rewards the curious. Hidden throughout the world are crafting recipes that are often tucked away in corners or behind environmental obstacles. Always look around every corner while questing, as these recipes are essential for high-level crafting.

Additionally, make it a habit to check your **Codex** frequently. As you defeat different types of enemies, you progress toward "Mastery" for that specific monster. Reaching mastery milestones grants free experience points, which can significantly speed up the leveling process during the early game grind.

If you find yourself with a surplus of armor or duplicate items, consider **salvaging** them instead of selling them for gold. Salvaging provides essential crafting materials that are often harder to come by than gold in the long run.

## FAQ

**Q: How do I get a mount in Farever?**

A: You can claim a free mount from the in-game shop. Once claimed, go to your collection menu, equip the mount, and press Shift to use it. This is a vital step covered in every **farever beginner guide** to ensure you don't waste time running.

**Q: What is the best way to get more inventory space?**

A: You should equip up to five pouches or bags. Small pouches add 6 slots, while Linen Bags (crafted by the Outfielder job) add 8 slots. You can also store excess items in the bank by talking to a Guild Merchant.

**Q: Why should I fight Sparkling Monsters?**

A: Sparkling Monsters are elite enemies that drop superior gear and unique rewards upon their first defeat. They are also a great source of specific upgrade materials if you choose to farm them.

**Q: When do I unlock the ability to use a second weapon skill?**

A: The Arsenal Slot unlocks at level 7. This allows you to equip a second weapon and use one skill from its kit, allowing for specialized character builds and playstyles.
