<Section icon={Shield} title="The Arsenal System and Gear Optimization">

In Farever, your gear is more than just a stat stick. The **Arsenal System** is the heart of any **farever abyss run** strategy. Every weapon you find has a unique moveset and four specific skills. By placing a weapon in your Arsenal slot, you can use its skills even if you aren't actively wielding it as your primary. This allows for "Hybrid" builds, such as a Warrior using Mage-style frost slows to control the battlefield.

<Callout type="warning" title="Weapon Level Warning">
Do not ignore lower-level weapons if they have superior skill packages. A Level 4 "Cheese Moon" Axe might provide better utility for an Abyss run than a Level 10 basic sword due to its boomerang combo skill.
</Callout>

### Essential Weapon Types for Abyss Runs
- **Shields**: Vital for the "Perfect Block" mechanic which reduces damage to zero without consuming endurance.
- **Bows**: Excellent for pulling single enemies out of dangerous "Abyss" packs in the open world.

<Section icon={Target} title="Step-by-Step Guide to Completing an Abyss Run">

Completing a **farever abyss run** requires a disciplined approach. Rushing into a dungeon without a plan is the fastest way to lose your progress. Follow these steps to ensure a high success rate in the Siagarta endgame.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Preparation and Consumables">
    Visit a **Cook** and an **Alchemist** in the local town. Ensure you have health regeneration food and endurance-restoring potions. Check your weapon durability; a broken weapon during a boss fight is a guaranteed failure.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="The Approach and Mob Pulling">
    Don't engage the entire dungeon at once. Use a bow or ranged spell to pull 2-3 enemies at a time. In the **farever abyss run**, enemies often have "Ominous" buffs that increase their damage if they are near other allies.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Navigating Vertical Hazards">
    Many Abyss instances feature platforming sections. Use your dash and double-jump to clear gaps. If you fall, you often take significant damage or respawn at the start of the section, losing valuable buff durations.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="The Boss Encounter">
    Every boss has a "Break" gauge. Use heavy attacks and specific weapon skills to deplete this. Once broken, the boss takes 50% increased damage. This is your window to use your high-cooldown burst abilities.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Extracting and Looting">
    After the boss falls, don't just leave. Check the "Hidden Wonders" usually located behind the boss arena. These often contain the rare materials needed for high-tier **Blacksmithing** recipes.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="info" title="Endurance Management">
During Step 4, never let your endurance bar hit zero. If you are exhausted, you cannot dodge or block, leaving you completely vulnerable to the boss's "Ultimate" attacks.
</Callout>

</Section>
