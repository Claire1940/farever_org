<Section icon={Target} title="How to Unlock and Capture New Companions">

While the first pet is a simple shop claim, the real depth of the **farever companions** system begins once you reach the second major region. To expand your collection beyond the basic starter, you must participate in a specific questline that introduces the capturing mechanic.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Complete the Initial Region">
    Progress through Skover Island and reach the first major city in the second region. This is where most advanced systems, including crafting and professional gathering, are introduced.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Locate the Companion Quest">
    Find the NPC associated with wildlife and companions. They will provide a quest that rewards you with the **Bug Net**, a specialized tool required for pet collection.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Equip the Bug Net">
    The Bug Net occupies a specific slot in your inventory. When equipped, it replaces certain combat interactions with capturing abilities, most notably the "Ground Pound."
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Find a Critter">
    Scour the environment for small animals like ladybugs or other "critters." These are often small and move quickly, so keep a sharp eye on the ground while exploring.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="The Capture Move">
    Approach the critter and use the Ground Pound ability. If timed correctly, the animal will be added to your collection of **farever companions**.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Section icon={Swords} title="Class Synergy and the Arsenal System">

In Far Ever, your choice of class—Warrior, Mage, Priest, or Rogue—dictates your combat style, but the **farever companions** and the Arsenal System provide the flavor and stat boosts needed for high-level play. The Arsenal System, unlocking at level 7, is particularly vital for progression.

| Class | Primary Role | Weapon Compatibility | Unique Mechanic |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Warrior** | Tank/Brawler | Swords, Shields, Spears | Dual-wielding swords |
| **Mage** | Ranged DPS | Staffs, Wands | Elemental Talent Trees |
| **Priest** | Healer/Paladin | Maces, Shields, Spears | Ally Damage Reduction |
| **Rogue** | Assassin/Archer | Daggers, Bows, Spears | High Critical Mobility |

The Arsenal System allows you to "slot" a weapon you aren't actively wielding. This provides 40% of that weapon's base stats and allows you to use one of its active or passive skills. For example, a Warrior might slot a heavy mace to gain a "Ground Pound" skill, which is also used for capturing **farever companions**.

<Callout type="info" title="System Insight">
The Arsenal System effectively allows for "multiclassing" through gear. A Priest can slot a spear to gain rogue-like agility skills, or a Rogue can slot a shield for extra survivability in solo dungeons.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Customization: Transmog and Runes">

Far Ever features an extensive transmog system that is remarkably player-friendly. Unlike many other MMOs in 2026, changing your appearance is free of charge once you have obtained an item. This applies to your armor, weapons, and eventually, the appearance of your **farever companions**.

**Key Customization Features:**
- **Automatic Unlocks**: Any item you pick up is permanently added to your transmog library.
- **Free Changes**: You can swap appearances at any time without spending gold or premium currency.
- **Skill Runes**: These are dropped in dungeons and change how your abilities function. For instance, a "Double Up" rune can add a second charge to your movement skills.

| Customization Type | Cost | How to Access | Impact |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Armor Transmog** | Free | Character Menu | Visual Only |
| **Weapon Skins** | Free | Character Menu | Visual Only |
| **Skill Runes** | Loot/Farming | Skill Tree | Gameplay Modifier |
| **Companion Skins** | Capturing | Companion Menu | Visual Only |

<Callout type="success" title="Success Tip">
Focus on farming "Sparkling Mobs" in the open world. These rare variants have a much higher chance of dropping rare runes and high-tier transmog items compared to standard enemies.
