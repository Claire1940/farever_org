## Maximizing Your Farever Best Start: Free Mounts and Pets

The moment you load into the game, your priority should be mobility and utility. Unlike many MMOs that gate transportation behind hours of questing, Farever allows you to secure a mount within the first five minutes. This is a cornerstone of a **farever best start**, as it significantly reduces travel time between objectives.

To claim your rewards, press **Escape** and navigate to the **Shop**. There are currently five items available for free that every player should grab immediately. Once claimed, you must manually equip them through your Collections menu (default key: **Y**).

| Item Category | How to Equip | Benefit |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Mount** | Collections (Y) -> Mount Tab | Increases movement speed; smooth transition to gliding. |
| **Pet/Companion** | Collections (Y) -> Companions | Follows the player; adds cosmetic flair. |
| **Consumables** | Inventory (I) | Provides temporary buffs or health restoration. |

## Understanding Weapon Skills and Synergies

One of the most unique aspects of Farever is that your power is split between your chosen class and your equipped gear. To guarantee a **farever best start**, you must understand that half of your active skills are determined by your weapon. There are over 25 different weapons in the game, ranging from fire scepters to two-handed swords.

When you gain experience, keep an eye out for a green blinking plus sign on your skill bar. This indicates that you can rank up your abilities. Increasing these ranks does more than just boost raw damage; it creates synergies. For example, some skills may apply a "slow" debuff, while other skills deal massive bonus damage specifically to slowed targets.

### Weapon Upgrading Strategy
While you can enhance your weapons at upgrade stations, it is generally recommended to save your rare materials during the early levels. The bonuses provided (such as +1 Faith or +1 Critical) are relatively minor on low-level gear. A key part of the **farever best start** involves hoarding these resources until you find high-tier weapons where the percentage-based bonuses, like a 5% chance to reduce cooldowns, become significantly more impactful.

## Fast Leveling: Map Completion and Codex XP

If you find yourself wondering where to go next, the map is your best friend. Farever uses a completionist system that rewards you heavily for exploring every nook and cranny of a region. Open your map and look for the gray areas and question marks. Completing these objectives provides a massive influx of XP.

Additionally, the **Monster Codex** (default key: **L**) is a vital tool for a **farever best start**. The Codex tracks how many of each enemy type you have defeated. Reaching specific milestones grants bonus XP that can help you outlevel the content you are currently facing.

| Kill Milestone | Reward Type | Strategy |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **4 Kills** | Small XP Boost | Target every new enemy type at least four times. |
| **8 Kills** | Medium XP Boost | Stay in an area slightly longer to hit this cap. |
| **20 Kills** | Maximum XP / Mastery | Best for farming areas with high enemy density. |

To reveal new map areas, look for the **Obelisks**. These are large stone structures with a beacon of light shooting into the sky. Interacting with them reveals the local geography and serves as your primary fast-travel network.

## Inventory Management and the Spark Recycler

Your inventory will fill up quickly with junk gear and "Lost Packages." Never discard these items. For a **farever best start**, you should travel to the second map's village hub and locate the **Spark Recycler**. Right-clicking your unwanted gear here converts it into crafting materials used for upgrades and professions.

For "Lost Packages," simply right-click them in your inventory to turn them into "Completed Packages." These can be handed over to the **Guild Merchant** for a flat reward of 100 gold each. This gold is essential for purchasing **Small Pouches**, which add six inventory slots each. You can carry multiple pouches, allowing you to stay in the field longer without needing to return to town.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Do not forget to use the Bank provided by the Guild Merchant. Storing items like Training Manuals (which grant weapon skill points) ensures you don't accidentally recycle or sell valuable progression items.

## Advanced Combat: The Arsenal and Runes

As you progress toward the current level 20 cap, you will unlock the **Arsenal** slot. This allows you to equip a secondary weapon that provides a passive or active benefit without needing to be your primary damage dealer. For instance, equipping a healing staff in your Arsenal slot can provide a consistent heal-over-time or a shield, which is crucial for squishier classes like the Rogue.

### Rune Customization
Runes are primarily found in dungeon end-chests or hidden world chests. These items are used to modify your class skills. Most active skills have rune slots that allow you to choose between different buffs:
*   Reduced Cooldowns
*   Increased Movement Speed
*   Raw Damage Multipliers

Always check your inventory for new runes after completing a dungeon. Equipping these immediately is a hallmark of a player with a **farever best start** mindset.

## Crafting and Underwater Exploration

Farever features six different professions, but you must pay gold to unlock each one. For most players, **Alchemy** is the best first choice. Since self-healing can be limited depending on your weapon choice, being able to craft Minor Healing Potions is a lifesaver. You will need to purchase vials from the Guild Merchant and collect herbs in the open world to maintain your supply.

| Profession | Primary Benefit | Key Material |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Alchemist** | Healing and Mana Potions | Herbs and Vials |
| **Chef** | Stat-boosting Food | Meat and Hunter's Sauce |
| **Blacksmith** | Gear and Weapon Enhancements | Ores and Scraps |

Finally, keep in mind that Farever handles water differently than most MMOs. There is no swimming; instead, you walk on the seabed. While underwater, you can double-jump much higher than on land, which is often required to find hidden chests or reach underwater objectives. Keep an eye on your blue oxygen bar—if it runs out, you will begin losing health rapidly. Using self-heals can help you "brute force" longer underwater sections if you are close to a goal.

## FAQ

**Q: What is the most important thing to do for a farever best start?**

A: The most important step is claiming your free mount from the in-game shop and equipping it via the Collections menu. This dramatically increases your efficiency in completing map objectives and reaching level 20.

**Q: Can I change the skills on my Arsenal weapon?**

A: Yes. By holding **Control** and left-clicking the weapon in your Arsenal slot, you can select which specific skill from that weapon you want to remain active as a passive or secondary ability.

**Q: How do I get more gold in the early game?**

A: The most reliable way to earn gold early on is by converting "Lost Packages" into "Completed Packages" and selling them to the Guild Merchant. Additionally, completing map objectives often rewards you with gold and valuable items.

**Q: Is there player trading or a Guild system?**

A: As of the current 2026 early access build, player trading and the Guild system are on the roadmap but not yet implemented. Players are encouraged to use the party system (up to 4 players) to tackle dungeons and bosses together in the meantime.
