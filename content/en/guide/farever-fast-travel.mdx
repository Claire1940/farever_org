## The Obelisk System: Your Primary Fast Travel Hub

In FarEver, Obelisks are massive, glowing stone pillars that emit a distinct beacon of light into the sky. These are not merely decorative; they are the most important landmarks for any traveler. When you enter a new zone, your first priority should always be locating the local Obelisk.

### How to Use Obelisks

Interacting with an Obelisk provides several critical functions. First, it clears the "fog of war" on your map, revealing the surrounding geography and points of interest. Second, it serves as a respawn point should you fall in battle. Most importantly, it is the gateway to the **farever fast travel** network.

| Function | Description | Benefit |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Reveal Map** | Interacting for the first time removes gray areas. | Identifies quest hubs and hidden chests. |
| **Set Teleportation Point** | Designates the specific Obelisk as your "home" return spot. | Provides a quick way to return to a safe hub. |
| **Fly to Obelisk** | Allows travel from one activated Obelisk to another. | Automated travel between distant regions. |
| **Respawn Point** | Automatically sets your death recovery location. | Minimizes travel time after a wipe. |

> **Warning:** Fast travel in FarEver is not "instant" in the traditional sense. When you select a destination Obelisk, your character transforms into a ball of energy and flies through the sky. While this process is automated, it does take a few moments to reach your destination.

## Securing Your Free Mount

While **farever fast travel** handles long-distance jumps, you still need a reliable way to cross the terrain between Obelisks and quest objectives. Fortunately, the developers have provided a way for every player to obtain a mount immediately upon starting the game.

### Steps to Claim and Equip Your Mount

Follow these steps to ensure you aren't stuck walking everywhere in the early levels:

1.  **Open the Shop:** Press the `Escape` key and navigate to the in-game Shop menu.
2.  **Claim Free Items:** Look for the starter bundle (often containing five items). Claim these for free.
3.  **Open Collections:** Press the `Y` key (default) to open your Collections menu.
4.  **Select Mount:** Navigate to the "Mount" tab at the bottom of the window.
5.  **Equip:** Select your new mount and click equip.
6.  **Hotkeys:** Go to Options > Hotkeys to bind a specific button for mounting and dismounting.

The transition between mounting and other movement mechanics, such as gliding, is exceptionally smooth in FarEver. You can seamlessly shift from a glide into a mounted sprint, which is vital for maintaining momentum during map completion.

## Map Completion and Exploration Rewards

The **farever fast travel** system is intrinsically linked to map completion. On the right side of your map interface, you will see a list of objectives for the current region. Many of these appear as gray question marks or shaded areas. Completing these objectives provides massive amounts of XP, often more than simple monster grinding.

### Exploration Mechanics Comparison

| Movement Type | Best Use Case | Limitation |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Obelisk Flight** | Moving between major regions/forts. | Automatic but not instant. |
| **Mounts** | Crossing open fields and roads. | Cannot be used in most dungeons. |
| **Gliding** | Descending from high peaks or Obelisks. | Requires height to initiate. |
| **Underwater Walking** | Reaching sunken chests and aquatic bosses. | Limited by the breath bar (blue bar). |

To maximize your efficiency, use the Obelisk to fly to the highest point in a zone, then use your mount and gliding abilities to reach specific question marks on the map. This "top-down" approach to exploration is the fastest way to hit the current level cap of 20.

## Underwater Traversal and Mechanics

FarEver handles water differently than many other MMORPGs. There is no traditional swimming mechanic; instead, your character walks along the floor of lakes and oceans. This is essential for certain map objectives and finding hidden chests.

> **💡 Tip:** Your time underwater is governed by a blue breath bar. If this bar empties, you will begin losing health rapidly. However, if you have a class with self-healing or have crafted high-quality healing potions through Alchemy, you can "brute force" your stay underwater by healing through the suffocation damage.

While underwater, you can still perform a double jump. Due to the buoyancy physics in the game, this double jump is significantly higher than a standard land jump, allowing you to reach elevated underwater platforms or escape dangerous aquatic predators.

## Enhancing Mobility through Class Skills

Your choice of weapon and class skills can also impact how you move through the world. While the **farever fast travel** system handles the macro-navigation, your active skills handle the micro-navigation.

*   **Weapon Skills:** Certain weapons, like daggers or staves, may offer dashes or blinks.
*   **Arsenal Slots:** You can equip a secondary weapon in your Arsenal slot. By holding `Control` and left-clicking the weapon, you can select specific passive or active skills that might boost your movement speed or provide defensive shields while you run past enemies.
*   **Runes:** As you complete dungeons, you will find runes that can be slotted into your skills. Look for runes that provide "Movement Speed" bonuses to your frequently used abilities to further optimize your travel time.

## Advanced Travel Strategies

To truly master the world of FarEver, you must combine all available tools. A common mistake new players make is ignoring the "Lost Packages" found during exploration. These can be converted into completed packages and turned in at the Guild Merchant for 100 gold. This gold is essential for buying inventory pouches, which allow you to stay out in the field longer without needing to fast travel back to a hub to bank your items.

### Inventory and Banking

| Item | Source | Capacity |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Small Pouch** | Guild Merchant | +6 Slots |
| **Standard Bank** | Guild Merchant NPC | Variable |
| **Spark Recycler** | Fort/Village Hubs | Salvages gear for materials |

By keeping your inventory expanded, you reduce the frequency with which you need to use **farever fast travel** to return to a merchant. Always salvage junk gear at a Spark Recycler instead of discarding it, as the materials gained are vital for late-game crafting and weapon upgrades.

## FAQ

**Q: Does it cost gold to use the farever fast travel system?**

A: Currently, traveling between Obelisks does not have a direct gold cost. However, you must first physically reach and interact with an Obelisk to unlock it for future use.

**Q: Can I fast travel while in combat?**

A: No. You must be out of combat and away from immediate threats to interact with an Obelisk or use the teleportation functions. If you are being chased, use your mount or a dodge-roll mechanic to create distance first.

**Q: How do I unlock more regions for fast travel?**

A: New regions are unlocked by following the main story objectives and reaching the border of your current map. Once you cross into a new zone, look for the beacon of the first Obelisk to establish a foothold in that region.

**Q: Is there a way to make the fast travel flight faster?**

A: The flight speed between Obelisks is fixed. To save time, ensure you are choosing the Obelisk closest to your actual destination rather than just the main hub of the zone. Additionally, ensure your "Set Point of Teleportation" is updated to your current questing area to minimize return trips.
